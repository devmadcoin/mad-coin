## Company Overview
**Founded:** September 23, 1889 (Kyoto, Japan)
**Founder:** Fusajiro Yamauchi
**Original Business:** Handmade hanafuda playing cards
**Current President:** Shuntaro Furukawa
**Headquarters:** Kyoto, Japan
**Global Status:** One of the most valuable companies in Japan, 5.4B+ games sold, 800M+ hardware units

## Historical Evolution

### Pre-Video Game Era (1889–1970s)
- 1889: Nintendo Karuta founded — handmade hanafuda cards
- 1950s–1960s: Diversified into toys, taxi services, hotels, instant rice, love hotels
- 1963: Renamed Nintendo Co., Ltd.
- 1966: Ultra Hand (toy) — designed by Gunpei Yokoi, massive success
- 1970s: Laser Clay Shooting System, Wild Gunman (light gun games)

### Early Electronic Era (1977–1985)
- 1977: Color TV-Game (first console, Japan-only)
- 1980: Game & Watch — portable LCD games, designed by Gunpei Yokoi
- 1981: Donkey Kong (arcade) — created by Shigeru Miyamoto, introduced "Jumpman" (Mario)
- 1983: Famicom (Japan) / Nintendo Entertainment System (NES, 1985 international)
- 1985: Super Mario Bros. — saved the video game industry post-1983 crash

### 16-Bit Era (1988–1996)
- 1988–1991: Super Famicom / Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES)
- Competed directly with Sega Genesis in the "Console Wars"
- Major titles: Super Mario World, The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past, Super Metroid
- 1989: Game Boy — portable revolution, designed by Gunpei Yokoi
- 1991: Super Mario World, F-Zero, Pilotwings (SNES launch)

### 3D Era (1996–2006)
- 1996: Nintendo 64 — Super Mario 64 defined 3D platforming
- 1998: The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time — widely considered greatest game ever
- 2001: GameCube — Luigi's Mansion, Super Smash Bros. Melee, Metroid Prime
- 2001: Game Boy Advance — 2D powerhouse
- 2004: Nintendo DS — dual screens, touch interface, massive success (154M units)
- 2006: Wii — motion controls, Blue Ocean strategy, expanded gaming to non-gamers (101M units)

### Modern Era (2011–present)
- 2011: Nintendo 3DS — glasses-free 3D, backwards compatible (75M units)
- 2012: Wii U — commercial disappointment (13.5M units), but produced classics
- 2017: Nintendo Switch — hybrid console/handheld, massive success (140M+ units)
- 2025: Nintendo Switch 2 announced

## Major Franchises

### Mario
- **Creator:** Shigeru Miyamoto
- **Debut:** Donkey Kong (1981, as Jumpman)
- **Own Franchise:** Mario Bros. (1983)
- **Main Series:** Super Mario platformers (2D and 3D)
- **Spin-offs:** Mario Kart, Mario Party, Mario Golf, Mario Tennis, Paper Mario, Mario & Luigi RPG, Luigi's Mansion
- **Characters:** Luigi (twin brother), Princess Peach, Bowser, Toad, Yoshi, Wario, Waluigi, Daisy, Rosalina
- **Sales:** 800M+ units across all Mario games — best-selling video game franchise in history
- **Design Origin:** Hardware limitations shaped iconic look: overalls (to show arm movement), mustache (no mouth pixels), cap (no hair animation), big nose (face readability)

### The Legend of Zelda
- **Creator:** Shigeru Miyamoto & Takashi Tezuka
- **Debut:** The Legend of Zelda (1986, NES)
- **Protagonist:** Link (not Zelda — Zelda is the princess)
- **Antagonist:** Ganon/Ganondorf
- **Key Titles:** Ocarina of Time (1998), Majora's Mask (2000), Breath of the Wild (2017), Tears of the Kingdom (2023)
- **Formula:** Action-adventure with puzzles, dungeons, open-world exploration
- **Innovation:** Non-linear design, music integration (Ocarina instrument), physics-based gameplay (BotW)

### Pokémon
- **Developer:** Game Freak (creators: Satoshi Tajiri, Ken Sugimori)
- **Publisher:** Nintendo + The Pokémon Company
- **Debut:** 1996 (Red/Green Japan)
- **Status:** Highest-grossing media franchise of all time (~$100B+)
- See: `2026-05-21-pokemon-franchise.md` for full breakdown

### Metroid
- **Creator:** Gunpei Yokoi & Yoshio Sakamoto
- **Debut:** Metroid (1986, NES)
- **Protagonist:** Samus Aran (one of gaming's first female protagonists)
- **Style:** Sci-fi action-adventure, exploration-focused ("Metroidvania" genre namesake)
- **Key Titles:** Super Metroid (1994), Metroid Prime trilogy (2002–2007, First-person adventure)
- **Producer:** Kensuke Tanabe (1986–2026, retired after Metroid Prime 4: Beyond)

### Kirby
- **Developer:** HAL Laboratory
- **Creator:** Masahiro Sakurai
- **Debut:** Kirby's Dream Land (1992, Game Boy)
- **Style:** Platformer, ability-copying mechanic (inhale enemies, gain powers)
- **Key Titles:** Kirby Super Star (1996), Kirby's Air Ride (2003), Kirby and the Forgotten Land (2022)
- **Partnership:** HAL Laboratory joint venture with Nintendo (Warpstar, Inc. → Nintendo Stars, Inc.)

### Donkey Kong
- **Debut:** Donkey Kong (1981, arcade)
- **Evolution:** Started as Mario's antagonist → became hero of own franchise (Donkey Kong Country, 1994)
- **Key Developer:** Rareware (DK Country trilogy)
- **Characters:** Diddy Kong, Dixie Kong, King K. Rool

### Fire Emblem
- **Developer:** Intelligent Systems
- **Debut:** Fire Emblem: Shadow Dragon and the Blade of Light (1990, Japan-only until 2003)
- **Style:** Tactical RPG with permadeath
- **Breakthrough:** Fire Emblem: Awakening (2013) saved the franchise from cancellation
- **Marth and Roy:** Introduced to West via Super Smash Bros. Melee before their own games released internationally

### Animal Crossing
- **Debut:** Dōbutsu no Mori (2001, N64, Japan-only) / Animal Crossing (2002, GameCube)
- **Style:** Life simulation, real-time clock, social interaction with animal villagers
- **Key Titles:** New Horizons (2020) — massive pandemic-era success (40M+ units)
- **Innovation:** Real-world time sync, non-competitive gameplay, player creativity (design, island customization)

### Splatoon
- **Debut:** Splatoon (2015, Wii U)
- **Developer:** Nintendo EPD
- **Style:** Third-person shooter — but ink-based territory control, not violent
- **Key Innovation:** Kid/squid transformation, motion controls, fresh aesthetic
- **Sequel:** Splatoon 2 (2017), Splatoon 3 (2022)

### Super Smash Bros.
- **Creator:** Masahiro Sakurai (HAL Laboratory)
- **Debut:** Super Smash Bros. (1999, N64)
- **Style:** Platform fighter — different from traditional fighting games (ring-outs instead of health bars)
- **Roster:** Crossover of Nintendo + third-party characters (Mario, Link, Pikachu, Sonic, Snake, Pac-Man, etc.)
- **Cultural Impact:** Nintendo's "who would win in a fight?" answered officially

### Star Fox
- **Debut:** Star Fox (1993, SNES) — first use of Super FX chip for 3D graphics
- **Protagonist:** Fox McCloud
- **Style:** Rail shooter with branching paths
- **Key Titles:** Star Fox 64 (1997, N64) — defined the series

### F-Zero
- **Debut:** F-Zero (1990, SNES launch)
- **Style:** High-speed futuristic racing
- **Status:** Dormant since 2004 (F-Zero GX), but Captain Falcon remains Smash Bros. staple

### Pikmin
- **Creator:** Shigeru Miyamoto
- **Debut:** Pikmin (2001, GameCube)
- **Style:** Real-time strategy/puzzle — command plant-like creatures

## Key People

### Shigeru Miyamoto
- **Role:** Creative Fellow (formerly Senior Managing Director)
- **Created:** Mario, The Legend of Zelda, Donkey Kong, Star Fox, Pikmin
- **Philosophy:** "A delayed game is eventually good, but a rushed game is forever bad."
- **Status:** Active at Nintendo since 1977, widely considered most influential game designer ever

### Satoru Iwata (1959–2015)
- **Role:** President of Nintendo (2002–2015)
- **Background:** Former HAL Laboratory programmer (Kirby, EarthBound)
- **Legacy:** Led Nintendo through Wii/DS golden era, "Iwata Asks" interviews, Nintendo Direct format
- **Famous Quote:** "On my business card, I am a corporate president. In my mind, I am a game developer. But in my heart, I am a gamer."

### Gunpei Yokoi (1941–1997)
- **Role:** Designer, General Manager of R&D1
- **Created:** Game & Watch, Game Boy, Metroid, Kid Icarus, Game Boy Camera, Virtual Boy
- **Philosophy:** "Lateral Thinking with Withered Technology" — using mature tech in novel ways
- **Legacy:** Died in car accident 1997, but philosophy still guides Nintendo hardware design

### Masahiro Sakurai
- **Role:** Game Director (left HAL Laboratory, founded Sora Ltd.)
- **Created:** Kirby, Super Smash Bros.
- **Current:** Still directs Super Smash Bros. Ultimate DLC and future projects
- **Style:** Known for exhausting perfectionism, works himself to health issues

### Kensuke Tanabe
- **Role:** Producer at Nintendo (1986–2026)
- **Known For:** Metroid Prime series, Paper Mario series, Donkey Kong Country Returns, Luigi's Mansion
- **Retired:** January 2026, final title: Metroid Prime 4: Beyond

## Business Strategy
- **Blue Ocean Strategy:** Don't compete directly — create new markets (Wii, DS, Switch)
- **First-Party Focus:** Nintendo's own games sell hardware; third-party support secondary
- **Innovation over Specs:** Underpowered hardware with novel interfaces (touch, motion, hybrid)
- **IP Licensing:** Very selective — protects brand integrity fiercely
- **Nintendo Direct:** Replaced E3 press conferences with direct-to-consumer video presentations

## Cultural Impact
- Saved the video game industry after 1983 crash (North America)
- Mario is as recognizable as Mickey Mouse globally
- Created multiple genres: platformer (Mario), action-adventure (Zelda), Metroidvania (Metroid), tactical RPG (Fire Emblem), life sim (Animal Crossing)
- Influenced countless developers and franchises
- "Nintendo Seal of Quality" — set standard for polished, family-friendly gaming
