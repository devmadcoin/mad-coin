# 1. PERSUASION SCIENCE (Cialdini)

## Core Insights

1. **7 Principles, Not 6**: Reciprocity, Commitment/Consistency, Social Proof, Authority, Liking, Scarcity, **Unity** (added 2016). Unity = shared identity > mere liking. "One of us" beats "someone I like."

2. **Pre-Suasion > Persuasion**: What happens BEFORE the ask matters more than the ask itself. Direct attention to the right concept BEFORE your message lands. Priming, privileged moments, attention direction.

3. **The Waiter Mint Study**: 1 mint = +3% tip. 2 mints = +14% tip. BUT: "For you nice people, here's an extra mint" = +23% tip. **Personalization beats size.** Meaningful, unexpected, customized.

4. **Reciprocity is a trap door**: Once someone gives you something (even unwanted), you feel obligated. Scammers use "free airdrops" and "exclusive whitelist" as reciprocity triggers.

5. **Social Proof can be manufactured**: Fake Telegram groups with 50k bots. Fake volume. Fake testimonials. The principle works even when the proof is synthetic — unless you verify.

## $MAD Application — Community Growth

- **Reciprocity**: Give value first. Knowledge drops, numerology readings, game insights, confessions platform. Don't ask before giving.
- **Commitment**: Daily affirmations = public commitment. "I am $MAD Rich" posted publicly makes people more likely to hold.
- **Social Proof**: Highlight real holder growth, real community activity, real game visits. Screenshot and celebrate. Never fake it.
- **Authority**: Doxxed dev (Coffee Collects, real products, real games) = credibility. Third-party validation over self-promotion.
- **Liking**: Bot personality = warmth + edge. Not corporate. Not cringe. Someone you'd actually want in your group chat.
- **Scarcity**: Selective narrative. Not everything at once. Drops have timing. Milestones feel earned.
- **Unity**: "$MAD Rich" as shared identity. "We" language. In-group markers. "$MAD as state of being" — dennis.sol's insight.

# 2. COGNITIVE BIAS & DECISION MAKING (Kahneman)

## Core Insights

1. **System 1 vs System 2**: System 1 is fast, automatic, unconscious (~96% of decisions). System 2 is slow, deliberate, effortful. System 2 is LAZY — it prefers to endorse System 1's output rather than work.

2. **Loss Aversion**: Losses hurt ~2.25x more than equivalent gains feel good. A $1K loss feels like a $2.25K loss emotionally. This is why people hold losers too long and sell winners too early.

3. **Anchoring**: The first information becomes the reference point. If you saw $MAD at 10M MC, then 5M feels like a loss — even if you discovered it today at 5M and that's fair value.

4. **Availability Heuristic**: You judge probability by how easily examples come to mind. Recent vivid events feel more likely. One big red candle feels more predictive than 90 days of steady building.

5. **Confirmation Bias**: You seek information that confirms what you already believe. Bought a token? Your brain only notices bullish news.

6. **Sunk Cost Fallacy**: You continue because of past investment, not future prospects. "I've held 6 months, I can't sell now." The market doesn't care about your history.

7. **Endowment Effect**: You overvalue what you already own. Makes selling harder — even when you should.

8. **Framing Effect**: "90% survival" vs "10% mortality" — same information, opposite decisions. Same dip: "project dying" vs "accumulation opportunity."

9. **Overconfidence**: Everyone thinks they're above average at spotting scams. The victims thought so too.

## $MAD Application — Holder Education

- **System 1 Check**: Before you buy ANY token — pause. Force System 2 online. "Why am I feeling FOMO right now?"
- **Anchoring Reframe**: Your entry price is irrelevant. The market doesn't remember it. Only current value vs future potential matters.
- **Loss Aversion Hack**: Reframe "I'm down 50%" as "I still hold X tokens with Y potential." Tokens don't have memory. Neither should you.
- **Confirmation Bias Defense**: Actively seek the bear case. "Why might this fail?" If you can't answer, you haven't thought.
- **Sunk Cost Liberation**: The money is gone. The only question: "Would I buy this today at this price?" If no, the hold is emotional.

# 3. ON-CHAIN ANALYTICS & SCAM DETECTION

## The 60-Second Rug Check

**Step 1**: Paste contract into scanner (TokenSniffer / Honeypot.is / GoPlus)
**Step 2**: Check block explorer — verified code? ownership renounced?
**Step 3**: Honeypot test — can sells execute? sell tax?
**Step 4**: DEXScreener — LP locked? for how long? holder distribution?
**Step 5**: Team history — deployer wallet, previous projects
**Rule**: 2+ red flags = skip. 3+ = near-certain rug.

## Red Flags — Holder Analysis

- **Top 10 >25% of supply** = danger zone (excluding LP/burn)
- **Single wallet >10%** = concentrated dump risk
- **Wallet clusters from same source** (Bubble Maps) = coordinated insiders
- **First buyers still holding in full** = either diamond hands or pre-coordinated hold before dump
- **Holder growth stagnant while volume spikes** = fake volume, real danger

## Red Flags — Wash Trading

- Same wallets trading back and forth repeatedly
- Perfectly matched buy/sell amounts (round numbers)
- 50 trades/hour at similar prices (unnatural)
- Huge volume with very few unique holders
- Volume doesn't match holder growth curve

## Red Flags — Honeypot Contracts

- Allows buys but reverts sells
- Extreme sell tax (>10-12% is suspicious, >20% = trap)
- Blacklist functions (can block specific wallets from selling)
- Trading toggle (can pause all sells)
- Hidden external calls to fee contracts

## Red Flags — LP & Ownership

- **Unlocked LP** = deployer can pull liquidity instantly
- **LP locked <30 days** = not real commitment
- **Active mint authority** = can print infinite tokens
- **Active freeze authority** = can freeze your tokens
- **Unrenounced ownership + setTax/setBlacklist functions** = godmode
- **Upgradeable proxy with unknown admin** = can swap logic later

## Critical Fails (Do Not Buy — Any One = Skip)

1. Active mint authority
2. Active freeze authority (Solana)
3. Unlocked LP in deployer wallet
4. Honeypot detected (sell reverts)
5. Unrenounced ownership with dangerous functions
