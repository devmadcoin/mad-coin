# The Art of Possibility — $MAD Study

**Authors:** Rosamund Stone Zander & Benjamin Zander  
**Published:** 2000  
**Core Premise:** Everything we perceive is invented. We can redraw the frame around any situation and create new possibilities.

---

## 12 Practices of Possibility

1. **It’s All Invented** — Our brains construct stories from selective sensory data. The map is not the territory. If you don’t like what you see, tell a new story.
2. **Stepping into a Universe of Possibility** — Move from scarcity/competition to abundance/contribution. The world of measurement traps you; the world of possibility frees you.
3. **Giving an A** — Assume everyone is already excellent. Give people an "A" as a gift, not a grade. Removes fear, unlocks potential.
4. **Being a Contribution** — Stop measuring success/failure. Ask: *How was I a contribution today?* Throw yourself into life as someone who makes a difference.
5. **Leading from Any Chair** — Leadership is not a title. It’s listening, supporting others’ growth, and taking responsibility. The conductor leads without playing a single note.
6. **Rule Number 6** — Don’t take yourself so seriously. Your "calculating self" worries about survival/status; your "central self" is connected to values and creativity.
7. **The Way Things Are** — Radical acceptance is not resignation. See reality clearly, then choose empowered action. Buddhist/Stoic roots.
8. **Giving Way to Passion** — Stop holding back. Wholehearted participation connects personal expression with universal purpose. Surrender, don’t force.
9. **Lighting a Spark** — Enrollment is not persuasion. Invite others into a vision that resonates with *them*. Genuine invitation beats obligation.
10. **Being the Board** — Stop reacting like a chess piece. Be the board itself — own the whole situation, including your role in it. Full accountability.
11. **Creating Frameworks** — Break downward spirals by restructuring meaning. Create visions that inspire unified action, not comparison or exclusion.
12. **Telling the WE Story** — Move beyond "you vs me" to "we." Shared humanity is the only durable foundation for collaboration.

## The Two Questions (Zander’s Framework)

When facing any challenge:
> *What assumption am I making, that I’m not aware I’m making, that gives me what I see?*
> *What might I now invent, that I haven’t yet invented, that could give me other choices?*
