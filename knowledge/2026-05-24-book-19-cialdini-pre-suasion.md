## The Core Thesis

**Pre-Suasion:** The process of arranging for recipients to be receptive to a message before they encounter it.

**Key insight:** What we present just before we deliver our message heavily influences how that message is received — often more than the message itself.

**The privileged moment:** The window of time when an audience is most susceptible to influence, right before the ask.

---

## Six Universal Principles of Pre-Suasion

### 1. PRIVILEGED MOMENT
**Timing is everything.**
- The moment BEFORE the ask matters more than the ask itself
- Create a "privileged moment" — a temporary state where people are more receptive
- **Example:** Asking someone to buy after they've agreed to something small (consistency)
- **$MAD Application:** Warm up the audience with a relatable pain point before introducing $MAD as the solution

### 2. CHANNELING ATTENTION
**What we pay attention to becomes important.**
- Direct attention to something → it becomes weighted more heavily in decisions
- "Attention is a limited resource. Where it goes, importance follows."
- **Single-chute question:** Ask a question that channels attention down one path
  - "Do you consider yourself an adventurous person?" → primes openness to risk
  - "Do you value community over everything?" → primes belonging-seeking

**$MAD Application:**
- X posts that start with a relatable question: "Tired of watching from the sidelines?"
- Channel attention to the FEELING of being left out → $MAD as the solution
- "Do you believe in community-built wealth?" → primes the $MAD frame

### 3. ASSOCIATION
**We respond to things based on what they're linked to in our minds.**
- Positive associations transfer to the target
- Negative associations transfer too
- **Spacial association:** Things that appear together are mentally linked
- **Temporal association:** Things that happen close in time are linked

**$MAD Association Strategy:**
- Link $MAD to: community, transparency, doxxed teams, real products, fun
- Never link $MAD to: scams, rugs, anonymous devs, promises of guaranteed returns
- The Roblox game is a positive association — "real product, real team"
- "Doxxed dev" = trust association

### 4. ANCHORING
**The first number mentioned becomes the reference point.**
- Price anchoring: Mention a high number first, then the real price feels cheap
- Social proof anchoring: "99 billion served" makes McDonald's feel inevitable
- **$MAD Application:**
  - Anchor with "Most tokens are rugs. $MAD is doxxed." → sets the trust bar high
  - Anchor with community size before price → "10K holders" > "$0.0001"

### 5. UNITY — The Seventh Principle (Beyond Influence)
**"We" is the most powerful word in persuasion.**
- Shared identity creates the strongest form of influence
- Acting together (synchronous movement, shared rituals) creates unity
- **$MAD IS UNITY:**
  - "$MAD fam" = shared identity
  - Daily affirmations = shared ritual
  - Community memes = synchronous culture
  - "We are $MAD Rich" = acting together

### 6. SELF-RELEVANCE
**We care most about things connected to our identity.**
- Messages that connect to self-concept are processed deeper
- "You are a $MAD holder" > "Buy this token"
- "I am $MAD Rich" = identity-level commitment

## The Importance of "Because"

**The word "because" triggers automatic compliance — even with weak reasons.**
- Xerox study: "Can I use the copier because I'm in a rush?" → 94% compliance
- "Can I use the copier because I need to make copies?" → 93% compliance (tautological reason still works)
- The word "because" activates the reason-seeking brain

**$MAD Application:**
- "GET $MAD because the doxxed dev builds in public."
- "GET $MAD because the fam is real."
- The word "because" makes the request feel reasoned, not pushy.
