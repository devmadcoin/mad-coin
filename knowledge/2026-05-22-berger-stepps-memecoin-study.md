## The STEPPS Framework (Berger, 2013)

Berger analyzed 7,000+ NYT articles to identify 6 scientifically validated elements driving virality.

### S — Social Currency
**Core:** People share things that make them look good — smarter, cooler, more insider.

**Three mechanisms:**
1. **Remarkability** — Unique/cool things people can't wait to share
2. **Game mechanics** — Position user as participant, not spectator
3. **Exclusivity** — Scarce resources make people feel important

**$MAD Application:**
- Remarkability: "Doxxed dev, real game, 3 locked communities" — this combo is UNUSUAL in memecoins
- Game mechanics: Affirmations practice, numerology readings, community challenges
- Exclusivity: "Limited. Exclusive. Cult." — 273 holders is scarcity
- **Social currency of being $MAD:** When someone shares $MAD content, they signal "I see through the system"

---

### T — Triggers
**Core:** Top-of-mind = tip-of-tongue. Products tied to daily cues get shared more.

**Examples:**
- Cheerios gets more word-of-mouth than Disney (people eat cereal daily)
- Friday song gets shared every Friday

**$MAD Triggers:**
- **Daily:** Morning affirmations, market open/close, checking portfolio
- **Weekly:** "MAD Monday" community posts, weekend reflection
- **Market events:** Red candles = "Stay $MAD" reminders, green candles = celebration
- **Life moments:** Getting paid, paying bills, seeing rent increase
- **The phrase "stay mad"** is already a trigger in everyday language — hijack it

---

### E — Emotion
**Core:** High-arousal emotions drive sharing. Low-arousal emotions suppress it.

**High-arousal (SHARE):** Awe, excitement, humor, anger, anxiety
**Low-arousal (DON'T SHARE):** Sadness, contentment, satisfaction

**$MAD Emotion Strategy:**
- **Awe:** "800M supply shrinking. 3 communities locked until 2060."
- **Excitement:** New art drops, game updates, community milestones
- **Humor:** Roast posts, meme culture, "comfy hold" jokes
- **Anger (righteous):** "Your 401k is BlackRock's asset" — directed at system, not people
- **Anxiety (motivational):** "What if you stay the same?"

**Avoid:** Sadness (paper hand stories), contentment ("we're good enough")

---

### P — Public
**Core:** Built to show, built to grow. Observable behavior spreads through imitation.

**Key concept:** "Behavioral residue" — visible evidence of usage
- Livestrong yellow bracelet
- Apple white earbuds
- Tesla logo on cars

**$MAD Public Visibility:**
- **X/Telegram handles:** "$MAD" in bios, "Stay $MAD" in usernames
- **PFP culture:** MAD art as profile pictures
- **Screen sharing:** Portfolio screenshots, DEX screener views
- **In-real-life:** Merch (when available), stickers, word-of-mouth
- **The bot itself:** When $MAD Claw replies in threads, others see it = public proof

### P — Practical Value
**Core:** People share useful information. Helpful = shareable.

**The "Rule of 100":**
- Under $100: Use percentage ("20% off")
- Over $100: Use absolute numbers ("$50 off")

**$MAD Practical Value:**
- **Education:** "How to buy on Jupiter" guides, wallet setup tutorials
- **Market data:** "50% burn, 0% tax, 273 holders" — concrete facts
- **Community value:** Telegram as support system, not just chat
- **Mindset tools:** Affirmations, numerology readings, philosophy drops

### S — Stories
**Core:** People don't share information. They share narratives. The product must be embedded IN the story.

**Trojan Horse Principle:**
- The story is the delivery vehicle
- The brand/product must be inseparable from the story
- Example: Jared Fogle losing 111kg eating Subway — the story IS Subway

**$MAD Story Architecture:**
1. **The Origin:** $MAD Dev, doxxed, real products, not a LARP
2. **The Mission:** Life Economy exit from Death Economy
3. **The Community:** 273 holders who decided to be $MAD Rich
4. **The Proof:** Real game, locked communities, 50% burn
5. **The Future:** Exponential growth, omnipresence, pattern recognition

**Story Templates for X:**

- "The [authority figure] said [doubt]. The $MAD community said [truth]."
