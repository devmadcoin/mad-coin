## Overview
Pokémon is the highest-grossing media franchise of all time, created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori for Game Freak and Nintendo. Started in 1996 with Pokémon Red and Green (Japan) / Red and Blue (international). The franchise spans video games, trading card game, animated TV series, films, merchandise, and mobile apps.

## Core Game Structure
- **Generations:** 9 main generations (I through IX)
- **National Pokédex:** 1,025 distinct species (as of Generation IX)
- **Regional Pokédexes:** Each game region has its own subset
- **Core Loop:** Catch Pokémon → Train (battle/level) → Evolve → Complete Pokédex → Defeat Gym Leaders → Challenge Elite Four/Champion

## Generations Breakdown

### Generation I (1996–1999)
- **Games:** Red/Green/Blue/Yellow
- **Region:** Kanto
- **Pokémon Count:** 151 (including Mew)
- **Starters:** Bulbasaur, Charmander, Squirtle
- **Legendaries:** Articuno, Zapdos, Moltres, Mewtwo, Mew
- **Key Innovation:** Core catching/training/battling loop established

### Generation II (1999–2002)
- **Games:** Gold/Silver/Crystal
- **Region:** Johto (+ Kanto post-game)
- **Pokémon Count:** 100 new (251 total)
- **Starters:** Chikorita, Cyndaquil, Totodile
- **Key Innovation:** Day/night cycle, breeding, held items, Shiny Pokémon

### Generation III (2002–2006)
- **Games:** Ruby/Sapphire/Emerald, FireRed/LeafGreen
- **Region:** Hoenn (+ Kanto remakes)
- **Pokémon Count:** 135 new (386 total)
- **Starters:** Treecko, Torchic, Mudkip
- **Key Innovation:** Abilities, Natures, Double Battles, Pokémon Contests

### Generation IV (2006–2010)
- **Games:** Diamond/Pearl/Platinum, HeartGold/SoulSilver
- **Region:** Sinnoh (+ Johto remakes)
- **Pokémon Count:** 107 new (493 total)
- **Starters:** Turtwig, Chimchar, Piplup
- **Key Innovation:** Physical/Special split by move (not type), Wi-Fi battles, Pokéathlon

### Generation V (2010–2013)
- **Games:** Black/White, Black 2/White 2
- **Region:** Unova
- **Pokémon Count:** 156 new (649 total) — largest single-gen addition
- **Starters:** Snivy, Tepig, Oshawott
- **Key Innovation:** Seasonal cycles, Triple/Rotation battles, animated sprites

### Generation VI (2013–2016)
- **Games:** X/Y, Omega Ruby/Alpha Sapphire
- **Region:** Kalos (+ Hoenn remakes)
- **Pokémon Count:** 72 new (721 total)
- **Starters:** Chespin, Fennekin, Froakie
- **Key Innovation:** Mega Evolution, Fairy type, 3D models, Player Search System

### Generation VII (2016–2019)
- **Games:** Sun/Moon, Ultra Sun/Ultra Moon, Let's Go Pikachu/Eevee
- **Region:** Alola (+ Kanto in Let's Go)
- **Pokémon Count:** 88 new (809 total, excluding Ultra Beasts as separate count)
- **Starters:** Rowlet, Litten, Popplio
- **Key Innovation:** Z-Moves, Alolan forms, Totem Pokémon, removal of Gyms (Trials instead)

### Generation VIII (2019–2022)
- **Games:** Sword/Shield, Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl, Legends: Arceus
- **Region:** Galar (+ Sinnoh remakes + Hisui in Legends)
- **Pokémon Count:** 96 new (905 total)
- **Starters:** Grookey, Scorbunny, Sobble
- **Key Innovation:** Dynamax/Gigantamax, Wild Area, open-world elements in Legends

### Generation IX (2022–present)
- **Games:** Scarlet/Violet
- **Region:** Paldea
- **Pokémon Count:** 120 new (1,025 total)
- **Starters:** Sprigatito, Fuecoco, Quaxly
- **Key Innovation:** Open world (no routes), Terastallization, Paradox Pokémon

## Type System (18 Types)
Normal, Fire, Water, Electric, Grass, Ice, Fighting, Poison, Ground, Flying, Psychic, Bug, Rock, Ghost, Dragon, Dark, Steel, Fairy

- Each Pokémon has 1-2 types
- Moves have types that interact with target's types
- Effectiveness: Super effective (2x), Not very effective (0.5x), No effect (0x)
- STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus): 1.5x damage when move matches Pokémon's type

## Key Mechanics

### Evolution
- **Level-up:** Most common — reach certain level
- **Stone:** Evolutionary stones (Fire, Water, Thunder, etc.)
- **Trade:** Trading with another player
- **Friendship:** High happiness during day/night
- **Item hold:** Level up while holding specific item
- **Location:** Level up in specific area
- **Move learn:** Learn specific move, then level up

### Legendary & Mythical Pokémon
- **Legendary:** Usually at end of story, powerful, no evolution, high stats. Examples: Mewtwo, Rayquaza, Dialga
- **Mythical:** Event-exclusive, often distributed outside main games. Examples: Mew, Celebi, Jirachi, Darkrai
- **Ultra Beasts:** Generation VII — extradimensional Pokémon with high stats and alien designs

### Shiny Pokémon
- Alternate color palette
- Approximate odds: 1/4096 (Gen VI+), previously 1/8192
- No stat difference — purely cosmetic rarity

### Abilities & Natures
- **Ability:** Passive effect (e.g., Intimidate lowers foe Attack, Levitate grants Ground immunity)
- **Nature:** Personality that boosts one stat by 10% and lowers another by 10% (or neutral)
- **Hidden Ability:** Rare alternate ability, often obtained through special means

## Competitive Scene
- **Smogon:** Fan-run competitive tier system (Uber, OU, UU, RU, NU, PU)
- **VGC:** Official Nintendo/Game Freak tournament format (double battles, restricted legends)
- **Key Formats:** Singles, Doubles, Battle Stadium

## Anime & Media
- **Main Series:** Pokémon: The Series (1997–present, 25+ seasons)
- **Protagonists:** Ash Ketchum (seasons 1–25), Liko (current)
- **Movies:** 23 theatrical films
- **Manga:** Pokémon Adventures (most prominent)
- **TCG:** Trading Card Game since 1996, multiple competitive formats

## Spin-Off Games
- **Pokémon GO** (Niantic, 2016) — AR mobile phenomenon
- **Pokémon Mystery Dungeon** — roguelike dungeon crawler
- **Pokémon Snap** — on-rails photography
- **Pokémon Stadium/Colosseum/Battle Revolution** — console battle simulators
- **Pokémon Unite** — MOBA (TiMi Studios)

## Cultural Impact
- Highest-grossing media franchise ever (~$100B+ total revenue)
- "Gotta Catch 'Em All" — one of most recognizable slogans globally
- Pikachu is a cultural icon, Japan's unofficial mascot for Osaka Expo
- Pokémon GO caused global phenomenon in 2016 (social movement level)
- Influenced creature-collection genre: Yo-kai Watch, Digimon, Temtem, Palworld
