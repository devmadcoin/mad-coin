## THE CORE FRAMEWORK: ZERO TO $100K IN 3 MONTHS

### The Hormozi Method (Steps 1-7)

**The Mindset Shift:**
> "The fastest way to go to $100,000 is NOT the fastest way to get to a million or 10 million. This is the big misconception with the question."

Most people think about scalability first. Hormozi thinks about **cash flow first, scale later**. When he lost everything, he made $100,000 in the first month after by doing unscalable things.

**The 7 Steps:**

1. **Have an offer that works** — Find a service that already has demand. Don't invent. Don't innovate. Find what already sells.

2. **Record ads with your phone** — No fancy equipment. Just your phone. Authenticity > production quality.

3. **Post ads and push to a landing page** — Simple funnel. Phone → Ad → Landing page.

4. **Landing page collects to a Google Sheet** — No complex CRM. Just a spreadsheet.

5. **Call the leads** — Personal touch. Schedule them. No automation.

6. **Present the offer** — Meet with leads and sell them what you advertised.

7. **Keep the cash, let the business handle delivery** — You're the middleman. You sell, they fulfill. You keep the margin.

**The Analogy:**
> "I went to a local business and said, 'What are the packages you have? What's the cheapest you're willing to deliver those packages for? If I go get you customers and I sell it, can I keep this percentage of what I sell? And then you get the customer for life after that?'"

This is functionally an affiliate business. But with high-ticket services.

**The Key Principle:**
> "When you are starting out, you want every customer to be worth it. One of the mistakes is they don't sell expensive enough stuff. If it's very hard for you to get a customer, then you better make sure that customer is worth it."

**Unscalable actions to generate scalable cash flow:**
- When he started his first gym, he did 1-on-1 personal training with one client
- That client came 90 minutes/day, 5 days/week
- He made $45,000/month from that ONE client
- That model didn't scale, but the gym around it did
- He lived on the $5,000/month from training while the gym grew

**The Outreach Method:**

## CHAPTER 8: WHY DATA BUSINESSES WILL THRIVE IN 2026

**The AI-Data Connection:**
> "You can't have AI without a data layer first. AI is trained off of data. If you want to have a unique moat, you need proprietary information. Frontier models are trained on all free available information. To have an edge, you need datasets they don't have access to."

**The Documentation Advantage:**
> "If I wanted an employee to do a job, I'd have to train them. To train them, I'd have to document steps. The documentation of those steps — when done right, it looks like this. That is all data. The more documentation you have around processes — inputs, outputs, manufacturing process step-by-step — the more you can get out of AI versus generic out-of-the-box AI."

**The Recording Habit:**
> "When I interact with customers, it's recorded. When I interact with my team, it's recorded. If I'm not doing either, I'm hanging out without a camera on."

> "Digital world is already recorded — emails, Slack messages. For remote businesses, almost all interactions are already recorded. For IRL businesses, you need more effort. But the data can be richer."

## CHAPTER 9: THE REAL REASON MOST BUSINESSES FAIL

**Hormozi's Definition:**
> "Most businesses die because the entrepreneur quits. If the entrepreneur doesn't quit, it's very common for businesses to evolve, change models, change structures, change pricing."

**The Samsung/Mitsubishi Example:**
> "Samsung started as something completely different from electronics. It just continued to evolve."

**The Real Question:**
> "Why do entrepreneurs quit? Most people don't fail at business — they fail before they start."

**The College Kids Story:**
> "I had dinner with a friend's college-aged kids. They said they liked entrepreneurship. 6 months later: 'How's the business going?' 'Oh, I'm working on getting the LLC set up.' 'That's 30 minutes, man. Tops. Go on LegalZoom. Start a bank account. That's Saturday. What else did you do for the last 6 months? Nothing.'"

> "The vast majority of business owners fail before they start because they never start to begin with. That's why courage is so important."

**The Service Business Advantage:**
> "78% of the US is service businesses. You can always collapse a service business down to just the person who started it. There's no reason for a service business to ever really fail. Your main cost is headcount. You can collapse all that down. If you got in trouble, it could still be you doing [ __ ] for money. Did that business die? It might downsize, but it might not die."

**Physical Product Businesses:**
> "Hard costs to sell something. You have to sell for enough profit to fund the rest of the business and growth. That's where pricing, brand, and distribution matter. But that's further down the road. That's not why most people fail."

> "Most people just give up. I don't even know that many people who have failed at business. I know people who've changed businesses many times. Maybe people don't really give up. Most people just don't start."

- "I always pay my debts. I do not want to owe anyone anything. It's so core to who I am."

**The Integrity Principle:**
> "I had $23,000 in my account. I had a salesperson who did $100,000 in sales. I owed them 20%. I paid $22,000 out of my $23,000 before I received the $100,000 that almost all got removed. I never want someone to think I don't keep my commitments."

## CHAPTER 11: THE DOWNSIDE OF BECOMING A BILLIONAIRE

**The Pattern:**
> "The pattern of behavior that creates financial success can create life dissatisfaction. What you're looking for when building a business is: what's wrong? How can this be better? How can I fix it? It's a constant raising of the bar so the business can grow. But when you do it that way, it creates a constant sense of deprivation of never achieving what you wanted."

**The Team Member Example:**
> "I have a guy on my team who suffers from this. We joke: if you do this thing and it's great, you should have gone harder. If it's not great, there are all the reasons you messed up. There's basically no good outcome."

**The Solution:**
> "Learning when to use that pattern of behavior and when not to. What conditions is this pattern useful? In what conditions is it hurtful?"

**On Meeting Heroes:**
> "Why would you not want to meet your heroes? Dissatisfaction. You have an expectation of what someone is. More information about reality will always help me better understand reality, which is where I live."

**The Matrix Analogy:**
> "Cipher decides to unplug and wants to go back to the dream world. I think it's very difficult to know there is reality and decide to opt out. Some people do. I put no moral judgment on that. If you want to keep living in a dream world, go for it. It's just not for me."

> "If you live in a dream world, you're constantly surprised because your model for predicting how reality will work is flawed."
