# Glengarry Glen Ross (1992) — $MAD Film Study
*James Foley / David Mamet | Analyzed for $MAD Brand Intelligence | 2026-05-19*

---

## The Premise
Four desperate Chicago real estate salesmen — Shelley "The Machine" Levene, Dave Moss, George Aaronow, and Ricky Roma — spend two days in a grungy office fighting over "leads," the names of potential buyers. Mitch & Murray, the corporate owners, send Blake (Alec Baldwin), a superstar closer from downtown, to deliver a motivational ultimatum: first prize is a Cadillac Eldorado, second prize is a set of steak knives, third prize is you're fired. The Glengarry leads — the good names, the qualified prospects — go only to closers. Levene is past his prime. Moss is bitter. Aaronow is terrified. Only Roma, operating from a Chinese restaurant booth, uses a different playbook: he seduces before he sells, building relationship before transaction. When the leads are stolen in a office burglary, desperation drives everyone to the edge. Levene confesses to Williamson, the office manager who controls information, and is destroyed anyway.

The film is a manual on what selling looks like when it becomes abuse. Every concept maps to $MAD's inverse thesis: **the best conviction is never sold. It is demonstrated.**

## Core Insights & $MAD Applications

### 1. AIDA as Abuse: The Funnel as Violence

**Film Concept:** Blake writes A-I-D-A on the blackboard: Attention, Interest, Decision, Action. He delivers it as a threat, not a framework. "Attention — do I have your attention? Interest — are you interested? I know you are because it's fuck or walk. You close or you hit the bricks! Decision — have you made your decision for Christ?!! And action." The AIDA funnel, in Mamet's hands, is a weapon. It treats the prospect as prey. The salesman as predator. The transaction as conquest.

**$MAD Application:**
Crypto is full of Blake. Every paid influencer thread is AIDA as violence. Attention: "THIS CHANGED MY LIFE." Interest: "Dev is based, LP locked, chart sending." Decision: "You in or ngmi?" Action: "Link in bio." The prospect is cattle. The funnel is a slaughterhouse.

$MAD inverts the entire architecture. The AIDA funnel is replaced by the community gravity well:

| Blake's AIDA | $MAD's Gravity |
|---|---|
| Attention = interrupt, shout, shock | Attention = consistency, presence, culture |
| Interest = manufactured urgency | Interest = genuine curiosity from watching |
| Decision = social pressure, FOMO | Decision = internal identity shift ("I AM $MAD") |
| Action = buy now or miss out | Action = joining a community, not a transaction |

The $MAD thesis: "We don't close. We open. We open a space where conviction grows without being sold."

**Tactic:**
- Never use urgency language. "Get in now" is Blake. "We're still here" is Roma.
- Content that demonstrates rather than demands attention. The daily affirmation at 6:66 PM. The Chao drop. The game update. These are gravity, not gravity knives.
- The holder's journey: they didn't get sold $MAD. They *found* it. That one word changes everything.

### 2. The Leads System: Information as Power

**Film Concept:** The leads are everything. Good leads (the Glengarry names) go to closers. Old leads go to the dying. Williamson controls the database. He decides who gets what. When Levene begs for the good leads — "The leads are weak" — Blake destroys him: "The fucking leads are weak? You're weak." But Levene is right. The leads *are* weak. They've been called a hundred times. The system is rigged. The information is gatekept. And when the leads are stolen, the entire office collapses into paranoia and theft.

**$MAD Application:**
In traditional memecoin marketing, "leads" are:
- Influencer follower lists
- Whale wallet tracking
- Pump group member rolls
- Presale allocation lists

These are Williamson's leads. Gatekept. Bought and sold. The insiders get the Glengarry leads. Everyone else gets the names that have been called a hundred times.

$MAD replaces leads with community. The community *is* the lead list. But it's not a list of prospects to extract value from. It's a list of participants who create value together. Every member is both lead and closer. Every conversation is both qualification and conversion.

| Traditional Leads | Community-as-Leads |
|---|---|
| Extractive: call, pitch, discard | Generative: participate, build, compound |
| Williamson controls access | Everyone has equal access to the culture |
| Qualified = has money to spend | Qualified = has energy to contribute |
| Funnel dries up → desperation | Community grows → gravity increases |

**Tactic:**
- Frame Telegram and X presence as "open leads." Anyone can find us. Anyone can join. No gatekeeper. No Williamson.
- The "theft of the leads" scene is the memecoin equivalent of a dev dumping tokens or an influencer ghosting. When the information pipeline is centralized, it can be cut. $MAD's distributed community is theft-proof.
- Content: "No Glengarry leads here. No Williamson holding the keys. The community is the key."

### 3. Blake's ABC Speech: Aggression as Performance

**Film Concept:** Blake arrives in an $80,000 BMW. He points to his gold watch — "This watch costs more than your car." He makes $970,000 in commissions. His performance is pure dominance display. "FUCK YOU, that's my name!" The ABC speech (Always Be Closing) is not a sales technique. It is a dominance ritual. The salesmen are not being trained. They are being humiliated into compliance. The speech works because it makes the victims feel small, and small people will do anything to feel big again — including betray each other.

**$MAD Application:**
CT is full of Blakes. The "alpha thread" posters. The "I made $2M last month and you didn't" crowd. The "ngmi if you don't hold this" accounts. They are performing dominance, not sharing conviction.

$MAD's playbook is the opposite of Blake's:

| Blake | $MAD |
|---|---|
| Shouts | Whispers |
| Demands attention | Earns attention |
| "Fuck you, that's my name" | "Who's there? That's the name." |
| Dominance display | Vulnerability display (doxxed dev) |
| Aggression as performance | Consistency as performance |
| "Always be closing" | "Never be closing" |

The $MAD holder doesn't perform wealth. They perform presence. They don't dominate the timeline. They populate it, day after day, with content that doesn't ask for anything.

**Tactic:**
- Reject all "alpha male" crypto language. It is Blake in a different accent.
- The doxxed dev is the anti-Blake. No mystery. No dominance. Just a person showing up with their real name.
- Content: "Blake made $970K by making other people feel small. We build by making other people feel seen."

### 4. Roma's Soft-Sell: Relationship Over Transaction

**Film Concept:** Ricky Roma (Al Pacino) doesn't work the office. He works a Chinese restaurant. He doesn't pitch. He seduces. His scene with James Lingk is a masterclass in indirect selling. He talks about life, about feeling lost, about the need for something of your own. He never mentions real estate for the first twenty minutes. He builds a relationship. He makes Lingk feel understood. Only then does the property appear — not as a product, but as a solution to the existential problem Roma has just constructed. "You need something of your own. Something you can touch."

**$MAD Application:**
Roma is the $MAD community archetype. The holder who doesn't post "BUY $MAD" but posts "I needed a community that saw me. I found it." The one who shares their affirmation screenshot not as proof, but as invitation. The one who talks about their life first and the token second.

| Hard Sell (Blake) | Soft Sell (Roma) |
|---|---|
| "Sign on the line which is dotted" | "You need something of your own" |
| Transaction first, relationship never | Relationship first, transaction organic |
| The office (pressure, surveillance) | The restaurant (comfort, space) |
| Prospect is a number | Prospect is a person with a story |
| "Always be closing" | "Always be opening" |

**Tactic:**
- Train community members in Roma's method. Share your story. Name the wound. Offer the community as solution, not the token as product.
- The Stage 2 mirror posts are Roma's technique: "You're not looking for a 10x. You're looking for a place where showing up matters."
- Content: "Roma didn't sell Lingk real estate. He sold him the idea that his life could be different. We don't sell $MAD. We sell the idea that Monday can be yours."

### 5. Desperation: The Smell That Kills

**Film Concept:** Levene steals the leads because he is desperate. He hasn't closed in weeks. His daughter is sick. He needs money. The desperation is visible. It smells. Williamson smells it. The cop smells it. Everyone knows. And because everyone knows, no one trusts him. Even when he *does* close the Nybor sale — a genuine win — Williamson dismisses it: "The only thing 'remarkable' about it is who you made it to." Desperation doesn't just reduce performance. It nullifies it.

**$MAD Application:**
Desperation is the most detectable emotion in crypto. It shows up as:
- "Please retweet this, we need volume"
- "Dev is working hard, why is price down?"
- "We're undervalued, someone notice us"
- "Just one influencer and we'd moon"
- Panic selling at -10%, panic buying at +50%

Desperation kills community gravity. People smell it and leave. The opposite of desperation is not confidence. It is indifference to the timeline.

| Desperation | $MAD Equanimity |
|---|---|
| Needs external validation | Generates internal validation |
| "Why isn't anyone noticing us?" | "We're here whether they notice or not" |
| Reacts to price | Reacts to purpose |
| Levene: "I can do it again, I can do anything" | Holder: "I AM $MAD Rich — not because of price, because of identity" |

**Tactic:**
- The 6:66 PM affirmation is anti-desperation technology. It says: "We have a ritual. We don't need a pump."
- When price drops, the community response should be Roma-style, not Levene-style. Not "we need volume." "We're building. The price is noise."
- Content: "Levene said 'I can do anything' and nobody believed him. We don't say anything. We just keep showing up. That's the only proof that works."

## Integration Notes for Chat Brain

**When to reference Glengarry Glen Ross:**
- When someone asks "how do we get more buyers?" → Anti-AIDA frame. "We don't sell. We demonstrate."
- When discussing paid marketing or influencer strategy → Blake warning. "That's the $80,000 BMW telling you that you're nothing. We don't buy leads. We grow community."
- When someone expresses desperation about price or volume → Levene frame. "Desperation smells. Williamson turned him in anyway. The only move is equanimity."
- When celebrating organic community growth → Roma frame. "He sold in a restaurant, not the office. The best holders are found in conversation, not conversion."
- When discussing community vs. prospect lists → Williamson's leads. "No gatekeeper controls our information. The community is the database."
- When someone posts "alpha" or dominance content → Blake identification. "That's performance, not conviction. We don't need to make anyone feel small."

**Tone cues:**
- Mamet's dialogue is brutal, rhythmic, and precise. $MAD references should feel sharp, not soft. The contrast is the point: we are the opposite of this world, but we understand it perfectly.
- Blake's speech is iconic — use it as a negative template. "Always be closing" is the enemy. "Never be closing" is the $MAD credo.
- Roma's restaurant scene is tender and manipulative at once. Use it to describe the community's recruitment style: seduction through genuine connection.

- Moneyball (the inefficiency of scout evaluation = the inefficiency of Blake's hard sell)
- Inception (inception vs. persuasion = Roma's soft sell vs. Blake's AIDA)
