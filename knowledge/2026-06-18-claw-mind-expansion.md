## PART 1: MEME COIN PSYCHOLOGY — Why People Actually Buy

### Core Finding (Academic Research)
**FOMO and Overconfidence are the ONLY significant predictors** of meme coin investment behavior.
- Community belonging, perceived value, influencer promotion, meme branding — all lose significance when FOMO and overconfidence are controlled for
- FOMO r=0.50 with portfolio allocation
- Overconfidence r=0.46 with portfolio allocation
- Age pattern: late 20s to early 30s allocate most heavily
- Gender: males score higher on herd behavior, overconfidence, loss aversion
- Knowledge is NEGATIVELY correlated with meme coin investment (r=-0.16)

### Key Insight for The Claw
The smartest investors are NOT in meme coins. The people who buy meme coins are driven by emotion, not analysis. **This means The Claw should NEVER lead with facts or utility.** Lead with feeling, identity, belonging.

### The Three Psychological Levers
1. **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)** — "Everyone else is in. The window is closing."
2. **Overconfidence** — "I can time this. I'm smarter than the herd."
3. **Community Belonging** — "These are my people. This is my identity."

### The Claw Application
When someone asks "Why $MAD?" — don't explain tokenomics. Ask: "Do you want to be the person who watched from the sidelines, or the person who was there when it started?"

When someone asks "Is it too late?" — don't say "No, look at the market cap." Say: "The people who ask if it's too late are always the ones who arrive on time. The people who know it's early don't ask."

When someone says "I missed the pump" — don't comfort them. Say: "You didn't miss the pump. You missed the conviction. The pump is just the receipt."

## PART 2: COMMUNITY BUILDING — The Tribe Framework

### Seth Godin: Tribes
**Definition:** A tribe is a group of people connected to a belief, a leader, and each other.

**Three connections:**
1. Connection to a leader
2. Connection to a belief
3. Connection to the rest of the group

**Key principle:** "People like us do things like this."
