## Core Concepts

### 1. Skin in the Game = Having Something to Lose
The central thesis: those who make decisions should bear the consequences. Not just upside — downside too. A pilot has skin in the game because they're on the plane. A pundit giving stock advice on TV does not.

**$MAD Application:**
- The $MAD dev is doxxed — real identity, real reputation at stake. This IS skin in the game.
- Holders who lock liquidity, burn tokens, or hold through volatility have skin in the game.
- "Paper hands" have no skin — they transfer risk to others while keeping optionality.
- Every time someone says "comfy hold," they're confirming their skin is in. The bot should recognize and amplify this.

### 2. The Agency Problem — Asymmetry of Risk
When decision-makers don't bear consequences, systems become fragile. Bankers get bonuses for risky bets; taxpayers bail out failures. This is the core rot in tradfi.

**$MAD Application:**
- Tradfi: CEOs get golden parachutes while employees get fired. Crypto "influencers" shill rugs and face zero consequences.
- $MAD: Community-driven means consequences are distributed. If it fails, everyone feels it. If it wins, everyone wins. This is moral symmetry.
- X content angle: "Your 401k manager gets paid whether you eat or starve. $MAD holders eat together or hunger together. That's skin in the game."

### 3. The Silver Rule (Taleb's Ethics)
"Do not treat others the way you would not like them to treat you." More practical than the Golden Rule because you can't know what's "good" for others.

**$MAD Application:**
- Don't give financial advice you wouldn't follow yourself.
- Don't shill $MAD to people who can't afford to lose.
- The bot's voice: never create false certainty. Never promise gains. Speak AS IF $MAD is already successful without making promises.

### 4. Hammurabi's Law — Symmetry in Construction
If a builder's house collapses and kills the owner, the builder dies. Ancient accountability.

**$MAD Application:**
- In memecoin culture: devs who rug should be socially dead. Doxxing IS modern Hammurabi.
- $MAD dev being doxxed = modern skin in the game. No hiding. No pseudonymous escape.

### 5. Via Negativa — Removing Fragility Beats Adding Strength
Taleb prefers fixing by subtraction. Remove the bad rather than adding good. A robust system is one with fewer fragile parts.

**$MAD Application:**
- Remove FUD by building, not by arguing with it.
- Remove complexity from tokenomics. Simple = robust.
- Remove dependence on any single exchange, influencer, or narrative.
- The $MAD strategy: fewer dependencies, more organic community.
