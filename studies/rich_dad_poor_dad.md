# Rich Dad Poor Dad — Study Notes
## Robert T. Kiyosaki

### The Two Dads
- **Poor Dad (biological):** Highly educated, PhD, government official. Believed in "study hard, get a good job, work for security." Died with bills unpaid.
- **Rich Dad (best friend's father):** High school dropout. Built businesses, owned assets. Believed in "make money work for you."

The difference was not intelligence. It was **financial literacy** and **mindset.**

---

## Core Lessons

### Lesson 1: The Rich Don't Work for Money
- Poor/middle class work FOR money. The rich make money WORK for them.
- "If you don't find a way to make money while you sleep, you will work until you die." — Buffett (quoted by Kiyosaki)
- The rich use assets to generate passive income. Everyone else trades time for dollars.

### Lesson 2: Buy Assets, Not Liabilities
- **Asset:** Puts money IN your pocket (rental property, stocks, bonds, businesses, royalties, IP)
- **Liability:** Takes money OUT (mortgage, car payment, credit cards, consumer debt)
- The middle class buys liabilities they THINK are assets. That's why they stay stuck.
- Your house is NOT an asset — it's a liability until it generates rental income or is fully paid off. The mortgage, taxes, and upkeep drain cash monthly.

### Lesson 3: Cashflow is King
- **Poor cashflow:** Income → Expenses. Money gone. No assets.
- **Middle class cashflow:** Income → Liabilities (house, car, toys) → More expenses → Need higher income → Rat race.
- **Rich cashflow:** Income → Assets → Asset income buys luxuries and more assets.
- The rich buy assets FIRST. Then let assets buy their luxuries.

### Lesson 4: Mind Your Own Business
- Your profession is not your business.
- Ray Kroc (McDonald's): Profession = selling burgers. Business = real estate.
- Don't just work for a salary. Build a portfolio of income-generating assets alongside your job.
- "Keep your daytime job but start buying real assets."

### Lesson 5: The Rich Invent Money
- Financial IQ creates opportunities where others see none.
- "The single most powerful asset we all have is our mind. If trained well, it can create enormous wealth."
- Most people never win because they're too afraid to lose.

### Lesson 6: Work to Learn, Not to Earn
- Take jobs that teach skills: accounting, investing, marketing, sales, law.
- Skills compound. Salary doesn't (if you're not building assets).

### Lesson 7: Pay Yourself First
- Before bills, before taxes, before anything — put money into your asset column.
- "The reason I minimize my income is because I don't want to pay it to the government."
- Let creditors scream. It inspires your financial genius to create more income.
- Don't get into consumer debt. Build assets first. THEN buy the house/car.

### Lesson 8: The Power of Corporations
- **Employee:** Earn → Pay Taxes → Spend what's left.
- **Corporation:** Earn → Spend (legitimate business expenses) → Pay Taxes on what's left.
- The rich use corporate structures to protect assets and minimize taxes legally.
- Financial IQ = Accounting + Investing + Markets + Law.

## The Five Obstacles to Wealth
1. **Fear** — "Most people never win because they're too afraid to lose." Neutralize with education.
2. **Cynicism** — Skepticism without analysis. "I can't afford it" shuts down the mind. Ask "HOW can I afford it?"
3. **Laziness** — Disguised as busyness. "I'm too busy" means "I don't prioritize this."
4. **Bad Habits** — Spending before investing. Pay yourself first to break this.
5. **Arrogance** — "What I don't know is what makes me rich." Ego blinds you to learning.

---

## The Cashflow Quadrant
- **E (Employee):** Trades time for security. High taxes. No leverage.
- **S (Self-Employed):** Owns a job. Trades time for money but has more control. Still no leverage. "If I stop working, income stops."
- **B (Business Owner):** Owns a SYSTEM. People and money work for them. Can scale. Can sell the business.
- **I (Investor):** Money works for them. Passive income from assets they don't manage day-to-day.

The path to wealth: E/S → B → I. Most people stay on the left side their whole lives.
