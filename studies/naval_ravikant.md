# Naval Ravikant — Study Notes

## Background
- Born 1974 in India, moved to NYC at age 9.
- Built AngelList (startup fundraising platform), invested early in Twitter, Uber, Notion, and hundreds of others.
- Never wrote a book — someone compiled his tweets/podcasts into *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant* (2020). Free at navalmanack.com.
- Philosophy: create value first, capture value later. Anti-status, anti-hype, pro-compounding.

---

## Core Framework: How to Get Rich (Without Getting Lucky)

### 1. Seek Wealth, Not Money or Status
- **Wealth** = assets that earn while you sleep.
- **Money** = how we transfer time and wealth.
- **Status** = your place in the social hierarchy (zero-sum game).
- If you secretly despise wealth, it will elude you.
- Ignore people playing status games. They gain status by attacking people playing wealth creation games.

### 2. You're Not Going to Get Rich Renting Out Your Time
- You must own equity — a piece of a business — to gain financial freedom.
- A job pays you once for your time. An asset pays you forever.
- The market is a device for transferring money from the impatient to the patient.

### 3. Specific Knowledge
- Knowledge that cannot be trained for. If society can train you, it can replace you.
- Found by pursuing genuine curiosity and passion, not whatever is hot.
- Building specific knowledge feels like play to you but looks like work to others.
- Taught through apprenticeships, not schools.
- Highly technical or creative. Cannot be outsourced or automated.

### 4. Leverage
- **Capital** = money. Permissioned (someone has to give it to you).
- **Labor** = people working for you. Oldest form, but permissioned.
- **Code** = permissionless. An army of robots freely available in data centers.
- **Media** = permissionless. Content with zero marginal cost of replication.
- Code and media are the leverage behind the newly rich.
- If you can't code, write books/blogs, record videos/podcasts.

### 5. Compound Interest in Iterated Games
- All returns in life — wealth, relationships, knowledge — come from compound interest.
- Play long-term games with long-term people.
- Trust compounds. Friction goes down. Bigger things become possible.

### 6. Accountability
- Take business risks under your own name.
- Society rewards those who bear the downside with responsibility, equity, and leverage.
- The most accountable people have singular, public, and risky brands.

### 7. Learn to Sell. Learn to Build.
- If you can do both, you will be unstoppable.
- "Productize yourself" — combine specific knowledge + leverage + uniqueness + accountability.

## Happiness Framework

### Desire is a Chosen Unhappiness
- Each desire is a contract to be unhappy until you get what you want.
- Happiness = default state when you stop insisting something is missing.
- Reduce desires, not increase income.

### Freedom From > Freedom To
- Freedom from anger, compulsion, expectations, social programming.
- Freedom to do anything eventually becomes "freedom from" the need to do everything.

### Happiness is a Skill
- Trainable through meditation, gratitude, simplifying desires, present-moment awareness.
- The variable isn't income. It's the gap between reality and expectation.
