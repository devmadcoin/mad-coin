# Psycho-Cybernetics — Study Notes
## Dr. Maxwell Maltz (1960)

## Background
- Plastic surgeon by trade. Noticed patients who got new faces/noses still felt ugly inside.
- Physical change ≠ psychological change. The self-image stayed the same.
- This led him to study the mind as a **goal-striving servo-mechanism** — like a guided missile.
- Published 1960. The bridge between Hill's "think and grow rich" and modern neuroscience.

---

## Core Concepts

### 1. The Self-Image is a Thermostat
- Your self-image is the **mental blueprint** of who you believe you are.
- It operates like a thermostat: if you see yourself as a $10/hour person, you'll unconsciously sabotage opportunities that pay $100/hour.
- **"We act and feel and perform in accordance with what we imagine to be true about ourselves."**
- External changes (new job, new face, new bag) don't stick unless the internal image changes first.
- **$MAD Application:** "$MAD Rich" isn't a wish — it's a thermostat reset. Until the self-image accepts it, the external reality can't hold it.

### 2. The Creative Mechanism (Success Mechanism)
- Your brain is a **goal-seeking servo-mechanism** — like a heat-seeking missile.
- Give it a clear target and it will course-correct automatically.
- **It doesn't judge whether the goal is good or bad.** It just follows instructions.
- Program it with positive goals = Success Mechanism.
- Program it with negative beliefs = Failure Mechanism.
- **$MAD Application:** The MAD Mind is literally this. Feed it conviction, it finds evidence of conviction. Feed it doubt, it finds evidence of doubt. The mechanism is neutral — the input determines the output.

### 3. The Theater of the Mind
- The subconscious **cannot distinguish between a vividly imagined experience and a real one.**
- Mental rehearsal (visualization) creates neural pathways identical to physical practice.
- Athletes use this. Musicians use this. High performers use this.
- **30 minutes daily**, alone, undisturbed: picture yourself acting successfully, feeling the sensations, experiencing the outcome.
- **$MAD Application:** "$MAD Abundant" said with feeling IS mental rehearsal. The repetition wires the self-image. The 21-day rule applies.

### 4. The 21-Day Rule
- Maltz discovered it takes **21 days of consistent practice** to form a new self-image habit.
- Not 21 days to achieve the goal. 21 days to rewire the thermostat.
- After 21 days, the new behavior becomes "automatic" — you act differently without trying.
- **$MAD Application:** The daily affirmation practice IS the 21-day reprogramming cycle. Every morning is a new imprint.

### 5. Let It Work, Don't Make It Work
- The success mechanism operates best when you **relax and trust it.**
- Too much conscious effort = tension = the mechanism jams.
- Set the goal. Let go. The servo-mechanism course-corrects automatically.
- Mistakes are feedback, not failure. Like a missile adjusting mid-flight.
- **$MAD Application:** Comfy hold is "letting it work." Panic selling is "making it work" through force. The mechanism needs trust, not micromanagement.

### 6. Dehypnotize Yourself
- Most people are hypnotized by their own negative beliefs.
- "I'm bad at math" "I'm unlucky" "I'm not the kind of person who..."
- These are delusions installed by past experience. They can be overwritten.
- Replace them through: relaxation + vivid imagination + repetition.
- **$MAD Application:** "I'm not a crypto person" or "Memecoins are scams" — these are hypnotic delusions. The $MAD fiction is the dehypnotization.

### 7. Happiness is a Habit
- You can acquire the habit of happiness by deliberately thinking pleasant thoughts.
- Each morning, give yourself suggestions: optimism, cheerfulness, anticipation of success.
- The nervous system reacts appropriately to what you think or imagine to be true.
- **$MAD Application:** Mad Morning isn't just a greeting. It's a deliberate habit-installation. "$MAD Abundant" primes the system for the day.
