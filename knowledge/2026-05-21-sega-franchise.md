## Company Overview
**Full Name:** Sega Corporation (Sega Sammy Holdings subsidiary)
**Founded:** June 3, 1960 (as Nihon Goraku Bussan / Service Games)
**Founders:** Martin Bromley, Richard Stewart
**Headquarters:** Shinagawa, Tokyo, Japan
**Current CEO:** Shuji Utsumi (Sega America/Europe)
**Status:** Third-party game developer/publisher (exited hardware 2001)

## Historical Evolution

### Origins (1940–1960)
- **1940:** "Standard Games" founded in Honolulu, Hawaii — coin-operated amusement machines for military bases
- **1951:** Renamed "Service Games" (SErvice GAmes = SEGA)
- **1960:** Nihon Goraku Bussan formed in Japan to import coin-op machines
- **1964:** Rosen Enterprises merged with Service Games → SEGA Enterprises

### Arcade Golden Age (1965–1985)
- **1966:** Periscope — first major hit, massive electro-mechanical submarine game
- **1969:** Acquired by Gulf+Western
- **1970s:** Early video games: Pong-Tron (1973), Blockade (1976)
- **1981:** Frogger (licensed from Konami) — massive global success
- **1982:** Zaxxon — first isometric shooter, pioneering 3D perspective
- **1983:** Astron Belt — early laserdisc game
- **1984:** Sega purchased by CSK Holdings (Japanese conglomerate); Hayao Nakayama became CEO
- **1985:** Hang-On — first game with motion-controlled cabinet (motorcycle)
- **1985:** Space Harrier — groundbreaking 3D sprite scaling

### Console Wars Era (1983–2001)

#### SG-1000 (1983)
- Sega's first console, Japan-only
- Released same day as Famicom (July 15, 1983)
- Outsold by Nintendo immediately

#### Master System / Sega Mark III (1985–1988)
- Japan: Mark III (1985), Master System (1987)
- International: Master System (1986)
- Better hardware than NES, but weaker third-party support
- Strong in Europe and Brazil (still manufactured in Brazil until 2021!)
- Key titles: Alex Kidd, Wonder Boy, Phantasy Star

#### Mega Drive / Sega Genesis (1988–1997)
- Japan: Mega Drive (1988)
- North America: Genesis (1989) — couldn't secure "Mega Drive" trademark
- Europe/Australia: Mega Drive (1990)
- **Sega's most successful console: ~30–40.8 million units**
- 16-bit powerhouse, competed directly with SNES in the Console Wars
- Key titles: Sonic the Hedgehog (1991), Streets of Rage, Phantasy Star II-IV, Shinobi, Golden Axe

**"Genesis Does What Nintendon't"** — iconic marketing campaign

#### Sega CD / Mega-CD (1991–1996)
- CD-ROM add-on for Genesis
- Expensive ($299), slow load times
- Key titles: Sonic CD, Snatcher, Lunar series
- Generally considered a commercial disappointment

#### Sega 32X (1994–1996)
- 32-bit add-on for Genesis
- Rushed to market, underpowered, confusing consumer message
- Widely considered one of Sega's biggest mistakes
- "The 32X was a disaster" — Sega of America employee

#### Sega Saturn (1994–1998)
- Japan: November 1994
- North America: May 1995 (surprise launch — alienated retailers)
- Powerful 2D hardware, complex 3D architecture (dual CPUs)
- Strong in Japan ( arcade ports, fighting games, RPGs)
- Struggled in West against PlayStation
- Key titles: Nights into Dreams, Panzer Dragoon, Virtua Fighter, Sakura Wars
- ~9.5 million units total

#### Sega Dreamcast (1998–2001)
- Japan: November 1998
- North America: September 9, 1999 (9/9/99)
- **Ahead of its time:** Online gaming built-in, VGA output, Windows CE compatibility
- Powerful hardware for its era, affordable ($199)
- Innovative VMU (Visual Memory Unit) — memory card with screen
- Key titles: Sonic Adventure, Shenmue, Crazy Taxi, Jet Set Radio, Soulcalibur, Phantasy Star Online
- **Why it failed:**
  - PlayStation 2 hype (DVD player + backwards compatibility + massive third-party support)
  - No EA Sports support (killed Western market)
  - Sega's reputation damaged by 32X/Saturn missteps
  - Piracy (GD-ROM easily cracked with boot discs)
- ~9.13 million units
- **Final Sega console**

### Third-Party Transition (2001–present)
- **January 2001:** Sega announces exit from hardware business
- **March 2001:** Dreamcast production ends
- **Post-2001:** Became multi-platform publisher
- **Key early multi-platform hits:** Sonic Adventure 2 (GameCube), Jet Set Radio Future (Xbox)
- **2004:** Sammy Corporation acquired Sega; formed Sega Sammy Holdings
- **Current:** Focus on PC, PlayStation, Xbox, Switch, mobile

## Major Franchises

### Sonic the Hedgehog
- **Creator:** Yuji Naka, Naoto Ohshima, Hirokazu Yasuhara
- **Debut:** Sonic the Hedgehog (1991, Genesis)
- **Character:** Blue anthropomorphic hedgehog, "The Fastest Thing Alive"
- **Rival:** Dr. Ivo "Eggman" Robotnik
- **Key Titles:** Sonic 2, Sonic 3 & Knuckles, Sonic Adventure, Sonic Adventure 2, Sonic Colors, Sonic Generations, Sonic Frontiers
- **Status:** Sega's mascot and most recognizable IP
- **Films:** Sonic the Hedgehog (2020), Sonic 2 (2022), Sonic 3 (2024) — box office successes
- **Cultural Impact:** Directly created to compete with Mario; defined "attitude" in 90s gaming

### Yakuza / Like a Dragon
- **Creator:** Toshihiro Nagoshi
- **Debut:** Yakuza (2005, PlayStation 2)
- **Style:** Action-adventure, open-world, dramatic crime storytelling with absurd humor
- **Protagonist:** Kazuma Kiryu (original series), Ichiban Kasuga (Like a Dragon onward)
- **Setting:** Kamurocho (fictionalized Kabukicho, Tokyo)
- **Innovation:** Blend of serious yakuza drama and comedic side content (karaoke, arcade games, hostess clubs)
- **Status:** Sega's most critically acclaimed modern franchise

### Persona
- **Developer:** Atlus (subsidiary of Sega since 2013)
- **Debut:** Revelations: Persona (1996, Japan)
- **Style:** JRPG + social simulation + dungeon crawler
- **Breakthrough:** Persona 3 (2006), Persona 4 (2008), Persona 5 (2016)
- **Themes:** Jungian psychology, masks, teenage identity, rebellion against society
- **Status:** Persona 5 is one of the highest-rated JRPGs ever

### Total War
- **Developer:** Creative Assembly (Sega subsidiary since 2005)
- **Debut:** Shogun: Total War (2000)
- **Style:** Turn-based strategy + real-time tactical battles
- **Status:** Premier PC strategy franchise

### Football Manager
- **Developer:** Sports Interactive
- **Style:** Soccer management simulation
- **Status:** Benchmark for sports management games

### Phantasy Star
- **Debut:** Phantasy Star (1987, Master System)
- **Style:** Sci-fi RPG series; Phantasy Star Online (2000) pioneered online console RPGs
- **Key Titles:** Phantasy Star I-IV (classic RPGs), Phantasy Star Online (Dreamcast), Phantasy Star Online 2 (2012)

### Jet Set Radio
- **Debut:** Jet Set Radio (2000, Dreamcast)
- **Style:** Cel-shaded skating/graffiti action
- **Innovation:** First major cel-shaded game; defined "cool Sega" aesthetic
- **Music:** Licensed electronic/hip-hop soundtrack — defining feature

### Crazy Taxi
- **Debut:** Crazy Taxi (1999, Arcade/Dreamcast)
- **Style:** Arcade driving — pick up fares, drive recklessly for tips
- **Music:** Offspring and Bad Religion licensed tracks
- **Status:** Iconic arcade experience

### Shenmue
- **Creator:** Yu Suzuki
- **Debut:** Shenmue (1999, Dreamcast)
- **Style:** Open-world adventure, QTE (Quick Time Event) pioneer
- **Innovation:** First fully open-world 3D game with day/night cycle, weather, NPC schedules
- **Budget:** $47–70M — most expensive game ever made at the time
- **Sales Disappointment:** ~1.2M units (couldn't recoup massive budget)
- **Status:** Cult classic, Kickstarter-funded Shenmue III (2019)

### Streets of Rage
- **Debut:** Streets of Rage (1991, Genesis)
- **Style:** Side-scrolling beat 'em up
- **Music:** Yuzo Koshiro compositions — legendary chiptune/electronic soundtracks
- **Key Titles:** Streets of Rage 2 (widely considered best beat 'em up ever), Streets of Rage 4 (2020 revival)

### Golden Axe
- **Debut:** Golden Axe (1989, Arcade/Genesis)
- **Style:** Fantasy beat 'em up
- **Status:** Classic co-op brawler, being revived as part of Sega's "Super Games" initiative

### Shinobi
- **Debut:** Shinobi (1987, Arcade)
- **Style:** Ninja action-platformer
- **Key Titles:** Revenge of Shinobi (Genesis), Shinobi III, PS2 Shinobi

## Key People

### Yuji Naka
- **Role:** Programmer, Producer
- **Created:** Sonic the Hedgehog, NiGHTS into Dreams
- **Status:** Left Sega 2006, founded Prope, later controversial (arrested for insider trading in 2022 related to Balan Wonderworld)

### Yu Suzuki
- **Role:** Game Designer, Director
- **Created:** Hang-On, Space Harrier, Out Run, Virtua Fighter, Shenmue
- **Status:** Legendary arcade innovator; still active, left Sega to work independently but remains associated

### Toshihiro Nagoshi
- **Role:** Former Sega CSO, Creator of Yakuza
- **Status:** Left Sega 2021, founded Nagoshi Studio under NetEase

### Naoto Ohshima
- **Role:** Character Designer
- **Created:** Sonic the Hedgehog character design, Dr. Eggman design
- **Status:** Left Sega, founded Artoon, later independent

## Sega's Console Philosophy vs Nintendo
- **Sega:** "Cool," edgy, arcade roots, speed, attitude, teen/young adult focus
- **Nintendo:** Family-friendly, innovation through hardware, first-party perfection, broad appeal
- **The Console Wars (1990s):** Direct cultural battle — Genesis = rebellious youth, SNES = wholesome family
- **Sega lost the war but won the culture:** Sonic remains iconic; Sega's "attitude" influenced gaming marketing forever

## Current Direction (2024–present)
- **CEO Shuji Utsumi:** "We are not a retro company. We really appreciate our legacy, we value it, but at the same time, we want to deliver something new — otherwise we'll become history."
- **No more mini consoles:** Officially confirmed no Saturn Mini or Dreamcast Mini
- **"Super Games" initiative:** Live service games, big updates, online social networks
- **Retro revivals:** Jet Set Radio, Crazy Taxi, Shinobi, Golden Axe being rebooted for modern platforms
- **Multiplatform focus:** Games on PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch, PC simultaneously
- **Films working:** Sonic the Hedgehog 3 performed well at box office (2024)

## Cultural Impact
- **"Sega!" scream:** Most iconic startup sound in gaming history
- **Console Wars:** Defined 1990s gaming culture; "Genesis Does What Nintendon't"
- **Arcade legacy:** Sega's arcade innovations (3D, motion controls, force feedback) influenced home console evolution
- **Dreamcast cult:** Despite commercial failure, Dreamcast has one of gaming's most passionate fan communities
- **Sonic as cultural icon:** Rivals Mario in global recognition; film franchise revived relevance
- **Yakuza revitalized Sega:** Modern critical darling, brought Sega back to prestige status
