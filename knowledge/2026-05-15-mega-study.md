## 8. Wyckoff & Trading Psychology

### Core Insights
- **Wyckoff's Composite Man**: Imagine one giant operator controlling the market. Whales, market makers, exchanges. They accumulate low, markup, distribute high, markdown.
- **Accumulation Phases**: A (selling climax) → B (range-bound, smart money buys) → C (spring/fake breakdown) → D (signs of strength) → E (markup)
- **Distribution Phases**: A (buying climax) → B (range, smart money sells) → C (upthrust/fake breakout) → D (signs of weakness) → E (markdown)
- **Three Laws**: Supply/Demand, Cause/Effect, Effort/Result
- **Spring**: Fake breakdown below support. Shakes out weak hands. Then reverses hard.
- **Upthrust**: Fake breakout above resistance. Traps late buyers.
- **Emotional Stages of a Trader**: Optimism → Excitement → Thrill → Euphoria → Anxiety → Denial → Fear → Panic → Capitulation → Despair → Depression → Hope → Relief → Optimism (cycle repeats)
- **Smart Money vs Retail**: Retail buys at the top (euphoria). Smart money buys at the bottom (despair).

### $MAD Community Application
- Teach holders to recognize WHERE we are in the Wyckoff cycle. Are we in accumulation? Markup? Distribution?
- The spring: When $MAD dips below a key level and everyone panics — that's the spring. Smart money is buying your fear.
- Emotional stages: When the community is at "despair" — that's the buy signal. When the community is at "euphoria" — that's the danger zone.
- Smart money buys when you're scared. They sell when you're greedy.
