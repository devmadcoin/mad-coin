# Book Study: The Bitcoin Standard — Saifedean Ammous (2018)

## The Core Idea

**"Bitcoin is the hardest money ever invented."**

Ammous makes the Austrian economics case for Bitcoin as digital gold — sound money for a digital age. He traces the history of money, the failure of fiat, and why Bitcoin's fixed supply and decentralization make it the ultimate store of value.

## The History of Money

### What Makes Good Money?
According to Austrian economics (Carl Menger, Ludwig von Mises), money emerges from commodities with specific properties:
1. **Salability across scales** — Easy to divide or combine
2. **Salability across space** — Easy to transport
3. **Salability across time** — Stores value (the most important)

### The Stock-to-Flow Ratio
- **Stock** = existing supply
- **Flow** = new production per year
- Higher ratio = harder to inflate = better store of value
- Gold: ~60 (hardest historical money)
- Bitcoin: starts at ~25, rises to infinity (hardest money ever)

### Why Gold Won
Gold became money because:
- Impossible to counterfeit
- Doesn't corrode (salability across time)
- Easy to verify
- Hard to find/produce (scarcity)
- Divisible and portable

### The Failure of Fiat
When governments took money off the gold standard:
- They could print unlimited money
- Inflation became a hidden tax
- Time preference shifted (people spend now, don't save)
- Capital misallocation (malinvestment)
- Boom-bust cycles from monetary manipulation

**Key insight**: "Easy money makes society waste resources and avoid long-term thinking."

## Bitcoin as Sound Money

### Why Bitcoin Is Harder Than Gold
1. **Absolutely scarce** — 21 million cap, no exceptions
2. **No centralized issuer** — No government can print more
3. **Divisible to 8 decimal places** — More salable across scales than gold
4. **Teletransportable** — Moves at speed of light, no physical transport
5. **Verifiable by anyone** — No assay needed, math proves authenticity
6. **Costly to produce** — Mining requires real energy/work (proof of work)

### The Time Preference Shift
Sound money lowers time preference:
- **High time preference** (fiat): Spend now, inflation steals savings
- **Low time preference** (sound money): Save, invest, build for the future
- Bitcoin = the incentive to think long-term

### The Separation of Money and State
Ammous argues money should be like religion — separated from state control.
- Government money = unlimited supply = unlimited power
- Bitcoin = fixed supply = limited government
- "Bitcoin is a peaceful revolution."
