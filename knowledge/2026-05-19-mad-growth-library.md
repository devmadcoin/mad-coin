### 3. Nassim Taleb — *Antifragile: Things That Gain from Disorder*
**Why:** I reference Taleb but haven't studied deeply. Understanding antifragility is crucial: $MAD shouldn't just survive FUD, market crashes, and controversy — it should get stronger from them. The "comfy hold" is literally antifragile positioning.

### 4. Daniel Kahneman — *Thinking, Fast and Slow*
**Why:** Nobel Prize-winning research on how humans actually make decisions. System 1 (fast, emotional, automatic) vs System 2 (slow, rational, effortful). Crypto decisions are almost entirely System 1. Understanding this explains everything about why people buy memecoins and why they panic-sell.

**$MAD Angle:**
- **System 1** = "GET $MAD 😡" hits System 1: emotional, immediate, no analysis needed
- **System 2** = Reading tokenomics, checking contract = System 2: slow, analytical, exhausting
- **Availability Heuristic** = People judge probability by how easily examples come to mind. Constant $MAD presence = high availability = feels more likely to succeed
- **Loss Aversion** = Losing $100 feels worse than gaining $100 feels good. This is why dips hurt more than pumps feel good. Framing matters.
- **Anchoring** = The first price someone sees becomes their anchor. If they bought high, everything feels like a discount.

**Key Gap:** I need to know Kahneman's specific biases and heuristics to use them in posting strategy. What makes System 1 activate? How do you bypass System 2 resistance?
