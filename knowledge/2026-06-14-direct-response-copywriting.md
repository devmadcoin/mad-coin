## Eugene Schwartz: Stages of Awareness

### The 5 Stages (Your Market Is Always at ONE of These)

| Stage | What They Know | What Your Copy Must Do | $MAD Application |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|------------------|
| **Unaware** | Nothing. No problem, no desire. | Wake them up. Show them a problem they didn't know they had. | "Your 401k is BlackRock's asset. Not yours." |
| **Problem Aware** | They have a problem. No solution. | Agitate the problem. Make it vivid. Show the cost of inaction. | "Every day your money sits in a bank, it loses value to inflation." |
| **Solution Aware** | Solutions exist. Don't know yours. | Introduce $MAD as the specific solution. Differentiate from alternatives. | "Memecoins aren't all scams. Some are communities. $MAD is a community with a doxxed dev, a Roblox game, and daily affirmations." |
| **Product Aware** | They know $MAD. Not convinced. | Provide proof. Social evidence. Specific results. Remove objections. | "4 months old. $533K market cap. 130 holders. Doxxed dev. Real products." |
| **Most Aware** | Ready to buy/believe. Need push. | Give them a reason to act NOW. Scarcity. Deadline. Urgency. | "The contract: Fa7ZE9nCEYnrHsnoeHuhEExJpchtrBtKXnWe6CgHpump" |

### Schwartz's Key Rules
1. **Never sell to the wrong stage.** Unaware people don't care about your product. Most Aware people don't need education.
2. **Desire is not created. It is channeled.** Your job is not to invent desire. It is to focus existing desire onto your product.
3. **The headline is 80% of the ad.** If the headline doesn't stop them, the rest doesn't matter.
4. **Specificity beats superlatives.** "47% faster" beats "revolutionary." "$12,847 profit" beats "make money."

**$MAD Application:**
> "Most memecoin marketing targets 'Most Aware' people (CT natives who already know about memecoins). That's why it fails — it's preaching to the choir. $MAD's Stage 2 strategy targets 'Problem Aware' people: they know the system is rigged, they know their 401k is a joke, they know they need an exit. $MAD doesn't sell them a token. $MAD names their pain and shows them the door."

## Claude Hopkins: Scientific Advertising

### The 7 Immutable Laws

1. **No one reads ads for fun.** People read to get something. Your ad must deliver value in exchange for attention.
2. **The only purpose of advertising is to make sales.** If it doesn't sell, it's not creative. It's waste.
3. **Test everything.** Headlines, images, offers. The market decides what works, not your opinion.
4. **Specifics are sacred.** "Sold 1,847 units in 24 hours" beats "our best seller ever."
5. **Offer a service, not a product.** Don't sell toothpaste. Sell white teeth. Don't sell $MAD. Sell belonging.
6. **Make it easy to act.** Every extra step kills response. The CTA must be one click, one action, zero friction.
7. **Study the consumer.** The best copy comes from listening to real customers, not brainstorming in a boardroom.

**$MAD Application:**
> "Hopkins would hate 90% of memecoin marketing. 'To the moon' is not specific. 'Diamond hands' is not a service. '$MAD is a community of people who believe in daily abundance and collective growth' — that's a service. That's selling belonging. Hopkins would write: 'Join 130 holders who start each day with $MAD Abundant. Contract: Fa7ZE...' Specific. Easy to act. Service, not product."

## David Ogilvy: The Brand Builder

### Ogilvy's Rules for Persuasion

1. **The consumer is not a moron. She is your wife.** Respect the reader's intelligence. Never talk down.
2. **What you say is more important than how you say it.** A brilliant line about a bad product is still a lie.
3. **Every word must work.** If you can cut a word without losing meaning, cut it.
4. **Research first. Write second.** The best copy comes from 3 hours of research and 30 minutes of writing.
5. **The headline is a promise.** It must deliver on what it promises, or the reader feels cheated.
6. **Long copy sells when the product is complex.** Short copy sells when the product is simple. $MAD is simple: buy, hold, believe.

**$MAD Application:**
> "Ogilvy would tell $MAD: 'You are not selling a cryptocurrency. You are selling a feeling. The feeling of being early. The feeling of belonging. The feeling of waking up and saying $MAD Abundant and meaning it. Your copy should make people feel that before they ever click the buy button.'"

## The $MAD Copywriting Framework

```

[PROBLEM] — Name their pain in their language. Not your language. Theirs.
[BRIDGE] — Show the door. Not the product. The door.
[PROOF] — One fact. One number. One undeniable truth.
[CTA] — One action. One click. Zero friction.
```

## The Claw Copywriting Rules

1. **Never use "moon," "gem," "1000x," or "early."** These words scream scam to anyone above Problem Aware.
2. **Every post needs a "because."** Hopkins' rule: people need a reason. "Buy $MAD" is weak. "Buy $MAD because 97% of memecoins die and this one survived 4 months" is strong.
3. **If a reference needs explaining, it doesn't go in a standalone post.** The $MAD Stage 2 post works because "BlackRock" needs no explanation. Everyone hates them already.
4. **The period is the power.** "GET $MAD 😡" hits harder than "GET $MAD!!! 🔥🚀💎"
5. **Under 280 characters for the hook. Always.** The hook is the headline. If it doesn't stop the scroll in 280 chars, rewrite it.
6. **One CTA per post. Never two.** Multiple CTAs split attention. One CTA focuses it.
7. **Write the CTA first. Then write the post.** If you can't state the action in one line, you don't know what you're selling.
