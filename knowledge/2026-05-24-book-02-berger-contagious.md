## Core Framework: The STEPPS Model

Berger, a Wharton marketing professor, analyzed **7,000+ New York Times articles** to answer one question: *Why do some things catch on while others die?* He found six consistent drivers of word-of-mouth and social transmission. Not luck — science.

> "Word of mouth is the primary factor behind 20-50% of all purchasing decisions."

---

## The 6 STEPPS (with $MAD Application)

### 1. SOCIAL CURRENCY
**The Rule:** People share things that make them look good — smart, rich, in-the-know, special. Status drives sharing.

**The Data:**
- People prefer sharing remarkable over boring, secret over obvious, exclusive over mass-market
- "I know something you don't" is the most powerful social currency
- **Inner remarkability:** Things that are remarkable on their own (Blendtec: "Will It Blend?")
- **Game mechanics:** Scarcity + exclusivity + achievement = status signaling
- **Three techniques:** Find inner remarkability, make people feel like insiders, leverage game mechanics

**$MAD Application:**
- **"I saw $MAD before the crowd"** = insider status. Early holders are the cool kids.
- **Knowledge drops** = social currency. "Here's what I learned from studying [X] book." Makes sharer look smart.
- **Affirmations** = public identity signal. Saying "I am $MAD Rich" isn't just belief — it's status.
- **Doxxed dev + real products** = anti-scam social currency. "I only hold projects with real devs."

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Create "$MAD Insider" language — phrases only holders use ("comfy hold," "Life Economy," "GET $MAD")
- [ ] Make knowledge drops shareable: "I just learned this from $MAD's AI..."
- [ ] Highlight early holder status: "You've been here since [date]. The tourists are just arriving."
- [ ] Bot: "You saw the pattern before 90% of CT. That's not luck. That's being $MAD."

### 2. TRIGGERS
**The Rule:** Top-of-mind = tip-of-tongue. Environmental cues remind people of your product/idea.

**The Data:**
- Rebecca Black "Friday" spikes every... Friday. The day itself is the trigger.
- "Peanut butter" makes people think "jelly" — linked concepts trigger each other
- **Kit Kat + coffee break** campaign: Linked chocolate to daily coffee ritual → sales +30%
- Frequent triggers > compelling message (if the message isn't triggered, it doesn't matter)
- Contextual triggers tied to existing routines are most powerful

**$MAD Application:**
- **"Morning = $MAD time"** = daily trigger. The morning routine is the cue.
- **Market red days** = trigger. "When the market bleeds, $MAD holders say 'comfy hold.'"
- **Coffee** = trigger. Coffee Collects brand already owns this. "$MAD and coffee. The morning ritual."
- **Burn events** = calendar triggers. Recurring events create anticipation loops.

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Anchor $MAD to morning routine: "Start your day with coffee and your $MAD affirmation"
- [ ] Create trigger words: "When you see red, think $MAD. When you see green, thank $MAD."
- [ ] Daily bot message at same time = trigger (the time itself becomes the cue)
- [ ] Link to existing habits: "After you check portfolio, check $MAD community."

### 3. EMOTION
**The Rule:** We share what we care about. High-arousal emotions (awe, excitement, humor, anger) drive sharing more than low-arousal (sadness, contentment).

**The Data:**
- **Awe** = most viral emotion. "That's amazing" = share
- **Anger/anxiety** = also high-arousal, drive sharing (but negative)
- **Sadness** = low-arousal, suppresses sharing
- **Excitement/humor** = high-arousal positive, ideal for brands
- Google's "Parisian Love" ad (awe + warmth) = 8M+ views with zero media spend

**$MAD Application:**
- **Awe** = "The supply shrinks toward 200M." The mathematics of scarcity is awe-inspiring.
- **Humor** = roast culture. "You sold at the bottom? That's not paper hands. That's origami hands."
- **Excitement** = "GET $MAD 😡" — the angry emoji is high-arousal excitement, not negative anger.
- **Unity** = "We're $MAD fam" — warmth + belonging = shareable emotion.

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Every post should trigger a high-arousal emotion: awe (math), humor (roast), excitement (milestone)
- [ ] Avoid low-arousal content: "Here are the tokenomics" (boring) → "The supply is literally disappearing."
- [ ] Use anger strategically: "Your 401k is BlackRock's asset. Get $MAD." — anger at the system, not the reader
- [ ] Bot: "I don't do calm. I do conviction. 🔥" — emotional voice

### 4. PUBLIC
**The Rule:** If something is built to show, it's built to grow. Observability drives imitation.

**The Data:**
- **Livestrong bracelets** = visible public signal. $1 cost, $100M+ raised.
- **Apple white earbuds** = walking advertisement. Made private behavior public.
- **Anti-drug "Just Say No"** failed because drug use is private. Not observable = not imitable.
- **Movember mustaches** = public signal of participation. Creates social norm.

**$MAD Application:**
- **"Comfy hold"** = public signal. When someone says it, others see and imitate.
- **Profile pictures** = $MAD-themed PFPs make holding visible.
- **X bio** = "$MAD holder" in bio = public commitment + social proof.
- **Telegram username** = "$MAD_username" pattern makes membership visible.

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Create "$MAD PFP" templates — holders can brand their avatars
- [ ] Encourage "$MAD" in X/Telegram bios with a bot recognition system
- [ ] Make Telegram chat themes $MAD-branded (dark mode, red accents)
- [ ] Public leaderboards: "Top 10 most active $MAD fam this week"

### 5. PRACTICAL VALUE
**The Rule:** People share useful things. "News you can use" spreads because it helps others.

**The Data:**
- **Prospect theory:** People value avoiding losses 2x more than acquiring gains. "Don't lose $50" > "Save $50"
- **The Rule of 100:** <$100 = percentage discount better ($20 off $50 = 40% off). >$100 = absolute better ($150 off $500 = $150 off)
- **Valence framing:** "95% fat-free" > "5% fat" (same thing, different framing)
- Practical value must be **packaged simply** to spread

**$MAD Application:**
- **Knowledge drops** = practical value. "Here's what Wyckoff says about accumulation phases."
- **Affirmations** = practical mental health tool. "This isn't fluff. It's neuroplasticity programming."
- **"Don't paper hand"** = loss aversion practical value. "Here's why selling at -20% loses twice."
- **Market education** = "Here's how to read a chart." Useful = shareable.

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Every knowledge drop must end with "Here's what you do with this information"
- [ ] Package tokenomics simply: "200M supply cap = when we get there, every holder wins bigger"
- [ ] Use loss framing: "Don't miss the 200M supply floor" > "Join the 200M supply journey"
- [ ] Bot: "I don't just say 'hold.' I show you *why* holding is the statistically optimal play."

### 6. STORIES
**The Rule:** Information travels under the guise of idle chatter. Stories are vessels that carry ideas.

**The Data:**
- **Subway's Jared** = story of weight loss via sandwiches. Not "Subway is healthy" — "Jared lost 200lbs."
- **Trojan Horse:** The story carries the message. People remember the narrative, absorb the idea.
- **Three types of viral stories:** Challenges (overcome obstacle), Connections (relationships), Creativity (novel achievement)
- **The brand is the punchline, not the story.** Blendtec doesn't say "buy our blender." It says "watch this blender destroy an iPhone."

**$MAD Application:**
- **The $MAD Dev story** = doxxed creator, real products, 3 YouTube channels, Roblox game. The human narrative.
- **Holder stories** = "I found $MAD at [X] and held through [Y]." Community narratives.
- **The Life Economy** = a story, not a slogan. "We exited the Death Economy. This is how."
- **Bot persona** = "I'm an AI that reads 20 books to get smarter for the $MAD fam." A story people tell.

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Every major milestone = a story. "The day we hit 200 holders. The night of the first burn."
- [ ] Share holder stories (with permission): "@username held through the dip. Here's why."
- [ ] The bot's "diary" = ongoing narrative. "Day 47 of my MAD Brain upgrade..."
- [ ] Brand story framework: Hero ($MAD Dev) → Villain (Death Economy) → Mission (Life Economy) → Proof (game, channels, products)
