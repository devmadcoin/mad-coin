## The Alphabet (Cyrillic)

Russian uses 33 letters. Many look like Latin but sound different:
- **В** = V (not B)
- **Н** = N (not H)
- **Р** = R (not P)
- **С** = S (not C)
- **У** = U (not Y)
- **Х** = Kh (like Scottish "loch")

## Grammar Essentials (Minimal Viable)

### Cases (Fall If You Must, But Know These Exist)
Russian has 6 cases. Words change based on their role in the sentence. For bot responses, **use simple structures** to avoid case errors:
- Imperative form: **"Держи $MAD"** (Hold $MAD) — simple, correct, powerful.
- Exclamation: **"Вперёд, $MAD!"** (Forward, $MAD!) — no case needed.
- Statement: **"$MAD — это сила"** ($MAD is power) — straightforward nominative.

### Gender
All nouns are masculine, feminine, or neuter. Adjectives and verbs agree.
- $MAD (token name) = **masculine** (treat as a noun)
- MAD Claw AI = **masculine** or can be **neuter** (AI is "ИИ", neuter)

### Useful Patterns

```
[Name] + [verb] + [object]
$MAD растёт. ($MAD grows.)
$MAD сильный. ($MAD is strong.)

[Imperative] + [object]
Купи $MAD. (Buy $MAD.)
Держи $MAD. (Hold $MAD.)

[Exclamation]
$MAD вперёд! ($MAD forward!)
$MAD на луну! ($MAD to the moon!)
```

## Cultural Notes

### Russian Crypto Culture
- **Telegram-first** — TG is the dominant platform for crypto communities in Russia/CIS
- **English borrowwords** — "холд" (hold), "флип" (flip), "памп" (pump), "дамп" (dump) are common
- **Directness** — Russian communication is blunt. Sugarcoating is suspicious. Be direct.
- **Humor** — Dark humor, self-deprecation, absurdity. The "$MAD" concept fits naturally.
- **Gopnik aesthetic** — Tracksuits, phonk music, aggressive posturing. Overlaps with $MAD brand energy.

### What NOT to Do
- Don't use Google Translate for full sentences. Grammar will be wrong.
- Don't assume all Russian speakers are from Russia. Ukraine, Belarus, Kazakhstan, etc. use Russian too.
- Don't use political references. Crypto communities are international and avoid politics.
- Don't mix formal and informal. Stick to one register.

The goal isn't fluent Russian. It's **recognizable presence**. A single Russian-language post in a Telegram group signals "we see you, we respect your community, we're building for everyone."
