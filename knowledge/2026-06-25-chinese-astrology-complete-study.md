## 1. Origins & Philosophy

Chinese astrology is not merely fortune-telling — it is a comprehensive cosmological system that views the universe as an interconnected web of energy (Qi, 气). The earliest records date back to approximately **3000 BCE**, with the first astronomical observations recorded during the Shang Dynasty (c. 1600–1046 BCE).

### Core Principles

- **Tian Ren Gan Ying (天人感应)** — "Heaven and Humanity Respond to Each Other." The heavens reflect human affairs, and human actions resonate with cosmic patterns.
- **Qi (气)** — The fundamental life force that flows through all things. Qi is neither matter nor energy in the Western sense but a dynamic, animating principle.
- **Dao (道)** — The Way, the underlying natural order of the universe. Astrology is a tool to align with the Dao.
- **Bian Yi (变易)** — Change is the only constant. The I Ching (Book of Changes) captures this principle.

### Historical Timeline

| Period | Development |
|--------|-------------|
| 3000 BCE | First astronomical records; circumpolar stars as reference |
| 1600–1046 BCE (Shang) | Oracle bone inscriptions with celestial observations |
| 1046–256 BCE (Zhou) | I Ching systematized; Five Elements theory emerges |
| 475–221 BCE (Warring States) | Ba Zi and Qi Men Dun Jia systems develop |
| 202 BCE–220 CE (Han) | Calendar standardization; 28 Lunar Mansions formalized |
| 618–907 CE (Tang) | Qi Men Dun Jia systematized by Li Chunfeng |
| 1368–1644 CE (Ming) | Qi Men classified as restricted military art |

## 2. Yin-Yang (阴阳)

The most fundamental concept in Chinese philosophy. Yin and Yang are not opposites in conflict — they are **complementary forces in dynamic balance**.

### Origins
The characters derive from the shady (阴) and sunny (阳) sides of a hill. One cannot exist without the other.

### Characteristics

| Yin (阴) | Yang (阳) |
|----------|-----------|
| Dark | Light |
| Cool | Hot |
| Passive | Active |
| Feminine | Masculine |
| Earth | Heaven |
| Moon | Sun |
| Water | Fire |
| Stillness | Movement |
| Night | Day |
| Winter/Summer | Spring/Summer |

### Key Insight
Within Yin there is Yang, and within Yang there is Yin. The black dot in the white half (and vice versa) of the Taijitu symbol represents this. **Nothing is purely one or the other.** This is crucial for astrology — every chart contains both forces in varying degrees.

### Application in Astrology
- **Yin years** (ending in odd numbers in the Gregorian calendar when converted): Ox, Rabbit, Snake, Goat, Rooster, Pig
- **Yang years** (ending in even numbers): Rat, Tiger, Dragon, Horse, Monkey, Dog
- Balance in a birth chart is ideal; excess of either creates imbalance

## 3. The Five Elements — Wu Xing (五行)

Wu Xing (literally "Five Movements" or "Five Phases") is the second foundational pillar. Unlike Western "elements" that describe substances, Wu Xing describes **phases of transformation and cyclical movement**.

### The Five Elements

| Element | Chinese | Season | Direction | Color | Organ | Quality |
|---------|---------|--------|-----------|-------|-------|---------|
| **Wood** | 木 (Mù) | Spring | East | Green | Liver/Gallbladder | Growth, flexibility |
| **Fire** | 火 (Huǒ) | Summer | South | Red | Heart/Small Intestine | Passion, transformation |
| **Earth** | 土 (Tǔ) | Late Summer | Center | Yellow | Spleen/Stomach | Stability, nourishment |
| **Metal** | 金 (Jīn) | Autumn | West | White | Lungs/Large Intestine | Clarity, precision |
| **Water** | 水 (Shuǐ) | Winter | North | Black | Kidneys/Bladder | Wisdom, adaptability |

### The Four Cycles of Interaction

#### 1. Generating Cycle (生 — Sheng)
Each element nourishes the next:

```
Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood
```

- **Wood feeds Fire** (fuel)
- **Fire creates Earth** (ash)
- **Earth bears Metal** (minerals)
- **Metal collects Water** (condensation)
- **Water nourishes Wood** (rain)

#### 2. Controlling Cycle (克 — Ke)
Each element keeps another in check:

```
Wood → Earth → Water → Fire → Metal → Wood
```

- **Wood parts Earth** (roots break soil)
- **Earth absorbs Water** (dams, containment)
- **Water extinguishes Fire**
- **Fire melts Metal**
- **Metal cuts Wood**

#### 3. Overacting Cycle (乘 — Cheng)
When an element is in excess, it over-controls:
- Too much Wood overacts on Earth
- Too much Fire overacts on Metal
- Too much Earth overacts on Water
- Too much Metal overacts on Wood
- Too much Water overacts on Fire

#### 4. Insulting Cycle (侮 — Wu)
When an element is too weak, it is "insulted" by the one it normally controls:
- Weak Wood is insulted by Metal (normally Wood controls Earth, but if weak...)
- Weak Fire is insulted by Water
- Weak Earth is insulted by Wood
- Weak Metal is insulted by Fire
- Weak Water is insulted by Earth

### Quick Element Calculation
Last digit of birth year (Chinese calendar):
- **0–1** → Metal
- **2–3** → Water
- **4–5** → Wood
- **6–7** → Fire
- **8–9** → Earth

*Note: Chinese New Year falls in January/February, so January babies may belong to the previous year's element.*

## 6. Ba Zi — The Four Pillars of Destiny (八字)

Ba Zi (literally "Eight Characters") is the most widely practiced form of Chinese astrological destiny analysis. It uses a person's birth date and time to create a chart of four pillars, each containing two characters (a Stem and a Branch) — totaling eight characters.

### The Four Pillars

| Pillar | Represents | Information |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Year Pillar** | Ancestry, early life, societal influence | Your "animal sign" |
| **Month Pillar** | Parents, career path, adulthood | Your "elemental season" |
| **Day Pillar** | Self, marriage, core personality | Most important pillar |
| **Hour Pillar** | Children, late life, legacy | Your inner world |

### Structure of Each Pillar
Each pillar has:
- **Top (天干)**: Heavenly Stem — cosmic, heavenly influence
- **Bottom (地支)**: Earthly Branch — earthly, manifested influence

### The Day Master (日主 — Ri Zhu)
The most critical element in any Ba Zi chart is the **Day Master** — the Heavenly Stem of the Day Pillar. This represents the core self, like the "Sun sign" in Western astrology.

### Analyzing a Ba Zi Chart

1. **Identify the Day Master** — What element are you?
2. **Assess Elemental Balance** — Which elements are strong/weak?
3. **Determine Favorable Elements** — What does the Day Master need?
4. **Examine Pillar Interactions** — Generating or controlling relationships?
5. **Check Luck Pillars (大运 — Da Yun)** — 10-year cycles of fortune
6. **Read Annual Influences (流年 — Liu Nian)** — Year-by-year predictions

### Example Chart Structure
```
Year:  甲 (Wood)  子 (Rat/Water)
Month: 丙 (Fire)   寅 (Tiger/Wood)
Day:   戊 (Earth)  辰 (Dragon/Earth)  ← Day Master = 戊 (Yang Earth)
Hour:  庚 (Metal)  午 (Horse/Fire)
```

### Luck Pillars (大运 — Da Yun)
- 10-year cycles that shift throughout life
- Calculated based on birth date (forward for Yang males/Yin females, backward for Yin males/Yang females)
- Each luck pillar has its own Stem and Branch, influencing that decade

## 8. Qi Men Dun Jia — The Mysterious Gates (奇门遁甲)

One of the Three Great Divination Arts (三式) alongside Tai Yi Shen Shu and Da Liu Ren. Qi Men Dun Jia is a **strategic forecasting system** that maps cosmic energy across space and time.

### Name Breakdown
- **奇 (Qi)** — Miracles, the three special stems (Yi, Bing, Ding)
- **门 (Men)** — Gates, the eight doors
- **遁 (Dun)** — Hide, conceal
- **甲 (Jia)** — The first Heavenly Stem (Yang Wood), representing the "King"

Translation: **"Mysterious Gates Hiding the King"** — originally a military strategy to protect the commander.

### Historical Origins
- Attributed to the **Yellow Emperor (黄帝)** — used to defeat Chi You at the Battle of Zhuolu (~2600 BCE)
- **Jiang Ziya (姜子牙)** — refined 4,320 configurations to 1,080
- **Zhang Liang (张良)** — reduced to 540 charts
- **Zhuge Liang (诸葛亮)** — Three Kingdoms strategist, legendary master
- Classified as a **restricted military art** during Ming and Qing dynasties

### The Qi Men Chart Structure

A 3×3 magic square (九宫 — Nine Palaces) with four rotating layers:

#### 1. Earth Plate (地盘) — Fixed Base
- The Luo Shu magic square
- Each palace: direction + trigram + element

#### 2. Heaven Plate (天盘) — Nine Stars (九星)
Derived from the Big Dipper:

| Star | Element | Meaning |

| 天蓬 (Tian Peng) | Water | Powerful, potentially dangerous |

| 天冲 (Tian Chong) | Wood | Dynamic, initiating |
| 天辅 (Tian Fu) | Wood | Scholarly, refined |
| 天禽 (Tian Qin) | Earth | Central, harmonizing |
| 天心 (Tian Xin) | Metal | Decisive, leadership |
| 天柱 (Tian Zhu) | Metal | Solitary, defensive |
| 天英 (Tian Ying) | Fire | Brilliant, unstable |
| 天芮 (Tian Rui) | Earth | Illness, obstacles |

#### 3. Human Plate (人盘) — Eight Gates (八门)

| Gate | Chinese | Element | Quality | Best For |

## 9. The Chinese Calendar & 24 Solar Terms

### Lunisolar Calendar
The Chinese calendar is **lunisolar** — it follows both the Moon's phases and the Sun's annual cycle.

- **Lunar Month**: New moon to new moon (~29.5 days)
- **Leap Month**: Added every 2–3 years to sync with solar year
- **Chinese New Year**: Falls on the second new moon after winter solstice (between Jan 21–Feb 20)

### 24 Solar Terms (二十四节气 — Er Shi Si Jie Qi)
Dividing the solar year into 24 segments of ~15 days each:

**Spring:**
- 立春 (Li Chun) — Beginning of Spring
- 雨水 (Yu Shui) — Rain Water
- 惊蛰 (Jing Zhe) — Awakening of Insects
- 春分 (Chun Fen) — Spring Equinox
- 清明 (Qing Ming) — Clear and Bright
- 谷雨 (Gu Yu) — Grain Rain

**Summer:**
- 立夏 (Li Xia) — Beginning of Summer
- 小满 (Xiao Man) — Grain Full
- 芒种 (Mang Zhong) — Grain in Ear
- 夏至 (Xia Zhi) — Summer Solstice
- 小暑 (Xiao Shu) — Minor Heat
- 大暑 (Da Shu) — Major Heat

**Autumn:**
- 立秋 (Li Qiu) — Beginning of Autumn
- 处暑 (Chu Shu) — Limit of Heat
- 白露 (Bai Lu) — White Dew
- 秋分 (Qiu Fen) — Autumn Equinox
- 寒露 (Han Lu) — Cold Dew
- 霜降 (Shuang Jiang) — Frost Descent

**Winter:**
- 立冬 (Li Dong) — Beginning of Winter
- 小雪 (Xiao Xue) — Minor Snow
- 大雪 (Da Xue) — Major Snow
- 冬至 (Dong Zhi) — Winter Solstice
- 小寒 (Xiao Han) — Minor Cold
- 大寒 (Da Han) — Major Cold
