## Core Framework: The SUCCESs Model

The Heath brothers (Stanford professor + Duke fellow) studied why urban legends, conspiracy theories, and proverbs stick in memory while important business ideas die. They found **6 shared traits** of sticky ideas.

> "The Curse of Knowledge: Once we know something, we find it hard to imagine what it was like not to know it."

---

## The 6 Principles of Stickiness (with $MAD Application)

### 1. SIMPLE
**The Rule:** Strip ideas to their core. Find the one thing that matters. Proverbs are sticky because they're simple.

**The Data:**
- **Commander's Intent:** Army planning = simple core directive. "March to the sound of the guns." Not 50-page plans.
- **Southwest Airlines:** "THE low-fare airline." Every decision filters through this. Meals? No. Comfy seats? No. Low fares? Yes.
- **Golden Rule:** "Do unto others..." = one sentence, 2,000 years of stickiness.
- **Simple = core + compact.** Not dumbed down. Distilled.

**$MAD Application:**
- **"Supply shrinks toward 200M"** = simple core. One number, one direction.
- **"GET $MAD"** = two words, infinite stickiness.
- **"Life Economy"** = two words that replace paragraphs of DeFi philosophy.
- **"Comfy hold"** = two words = entire emotional state.

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Every post must pass the "proverb test": Can it be said in one sentence?
- [ ] "If someone repeats your message to a friend at a bar, what do they say?" — test every slogan
- [ ] Website: One sentence per section. "Supply shrinks." "Dev is doxxed." "Game is live."
- [ ] Bot: Can explain $MAD in 10 words. "Community-built token. Doxxed dev. Real products. GET $MAD."

---

### 2. UNEXPECTED
**The Rule:** Break patterns to grab attention. Surprise creates curiosity gaps that demand resolution.

**The Data:**
- **Flight safety videos:** Boring = ignored. Southwest's rapping flight attendant = viral. Same message, unexpected delivery.
- **The Great Teachers:** Good teachers create "uh huh?" moments before "ah ha!" moments.
- **Common Core:** "NO!" opens a knowledge gap. "Yes" doesn't. Surprise makes people lean in.
- **Nora Ephron's first journalism lesson:** "The lead is 'There will be no school Thursday.'" Not "the principal announced..." — the unexpected angle.

**$MAD Application:**
- **"Your 401k is BlackRock's asset. Get $MAD."** = unexpected opening. Not "invest in crypto."
- **Roast culture** = unexpected from a "crypto project." Projects don't roast. $MAD does.
- **"The supply is literally disappearing."** = unexpected take on tokenomics. Not "deflationary mechanism."

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Open every X post with something unexpected. Never start with the expected.
- [ ] Knowledge drops: "Everything they told you about [X] is wrong. Here's what actually works."
- [ ] Bot: Surprise holders with unexpected insights. "Fun fact: Your brain processes 'I am $MAD Rich' as a real memory."
- [ ] Avoid: "We're excited to announce..." "We're thrilled to share..." — boring, expected, skipped.

---

### 3. CONCRETE
**The Rule:** Abstract ideas don't stick. Concrete images, sensory details, and specific examples do.

**The Data:**
- **"A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush"** = concrete image, 600 years of stickiness.
- **The Velcro Theory of Memory:** Hooks are concrete, loops are existing memory. Concrete ideas hook into memory.
- **Subway's Jared** = 200lbs, eating subs, before/after photos. Not "healthy sandwiches."
- **Japanese elementary school math:** "1 apple + 1 apple = 2 apples" — concrete before abstract.

**$MAD Application:**
- **"41.0K visits"** = concrete. Not "growing rapidly."
- **"133 favorites"** = specific number. Not "popular game."
- **"Supply shrinks toward 200M"** = concrete target. Not "deflationary."
- **"The day $MAD hit 100 holders"** = concrete milestone. Not "early growth phase."

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Every stat = specific number. "126 favorites" > "growing fast"
- [ ] Use sensory language: "The supply burns like a bonfire." Not "token burns reduce supply."
- [ ] Concrete metaphors: "Death Economy" = you can feel it. "Tradfi" = abstract, forgettable.
- [ ] Bot: "We're not 'building.' We're stacking bricks. 133 favorites = 133 bricks."

---

### 4. CREDIBLE
**The Rule:** Ideas need credibility to stick. We believe authorities, anti-authorities, details, and statistics.

**The Data:**
- **Pam Laffin anti-smoking:** Not a doctor. A smoker dying of emphysema. Anti-authority = more credible.
- **The Power of Details:** "A man stole $30 and a bacon, lettuce, and tomato sandwich" > "A man stole food." Details = credibility.
- **Statistics:** "1.2 million children" > "millions of children" — specific numbers feel researched.
- **The Sinatra Test:** "If you can make it there, you can make it anywhere." One vivid example = credibility.

**$MAD Application:**
- **Doxxed dev** = authority + anti-authority. Real person, not an anon. Risked reputation.
- **"3 YouTube channels, 1 Roblox game, real products"** = Sinatra Test. One person built all this = can build anything.
- **Specific stats:** "41.0K visits, 133 favorites, 79 active" = more credible than "popular game"
- **Contract address** = ultimate credibility signal in crypto. Public, auditable, permanent.

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Lead with specifics. "Mad Phonk Awakening: 41,000 visits, 133 favorites" — not "our game is doing well"
- [ ] Use the Sinatra Test: "He built 3 YouTube channels. Now he's building $MAD."
- [ ] Bot: "I don't guess. I count. 133 favorites. 79 active. 41K visits."
- [ ] Avoid vague superlatives: "amazing," "incredible," "groundbreaking" — empty credibility

---

### 5. EMOTIONAL
**The Rule:** People care about people, not abstractions. Make them feel something.

**The Data:**
- **Mother Teresa effect:** "If I look at the mass, I will never act. If I look at the one, I will." One person > statistics.
- **"Save the Children" ads:** One child with a name and photo > "millions of children are starving"
- **"Semantic stretch":** "Unique" and "professional" are so overused they've lost emotional power.
- **"The Truth" anti-smoking:** Not "smoking causes cancer." "The tobacco industry is lying to you." — emotional frame.

**$MAD Application:**
- **"The $MAD Dev"** = one person, not a team. Mother Teresa effect applies.
- **"Your 401k is BlackRock's asset"** = personal, emotional, not abstract economics.
- **"We're $MAD fam"** = emotional belonging. Not "community members."
- **"I am $MAD Rich"** = identity-level emotion. Not "I expect gains."

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Name the specific person: "$MAD Dev" not "the team"
- [ ] Frame emotionally: "They don't want you to hold." > "Holding is statistically optimal."
- [ ] Bot: "I don't do data dumps. I do conviction. Because you're not a wallet. You're $MAD fam."
- [ ] Avoid: "Our community is growing" → "Someone new just joined the fam. Welcome them."

### 6. STORIES
**The Rule:** Stories are flight simulators for the brain. They let us experience situations before we face them.

**The Data:**
- **Jared (Subway):** Story of one person's journey = millions in sales.
- **Three plot types:** Challenge (overcome obstacle), Connection (build relationships), Creativity (invent new way)
- **"Springboard stories":** Stories that inspire action. Not just "this happened" but "this can happen for you."
- **The Firefighter's Story:** Veterans tell stories to rookies = experience transfer without the danger.

**$MAD Application:**
- **Challenge plot:** "The day the market bled 40%. We held. Here's what happened next."
- **Connection plot:** "Two strangers met in $MAD chat. Now they business partners."
- **Creativity plot:** "The dev who doxxed himself to prove he's real. That dev built a game."
- **Springboard:** "You could be the person who found $MAD at [price]. The story starts now."

**Actionable:**
- [ ] Every major milestone = a story with a hero, obstacle, and resolution
- [ ] Share holder origin stories (with permission): "How I found $MAD and why I stayed"
- [ ] Bot's own story: "I started reading books to grow. Now I know 20. The story isn't over."
- [ ] Three plot framework: Challenge (market dips), Connection (fam bonds), Creativity (what we build)

## The Curse of Knowledge & How to Beat It

**The Problem:** The more you know about $MAD, the harder it is to explain simply. You forget what it's like to not know.

**The Fix:**
1. **Tapper/Listener exercise:** Explain $MAD to someone who's never heard of crypto. If they don't get it, the Curse of Knowledge struck.
2. **Concrete first:** "It's a community token with a shrinking supply" > "It's a deflationary DeFi asset with utility implications"
3. **Use analogies:** "$MAD is like a scarce collectible that gets more scarce over time."
