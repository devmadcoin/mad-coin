# Book Study: The Richest Man in Babylon — George S. Clason (1926)

## The Core Idea

**"A part of all I earn is mine to keep."**

Clason distills timeless wealth principles through parables set in ancient Babylon. The central message: wealth is not about luck or genius. It's about **simple, disciplined habits** applied consistently over time.

## The Seven Cures for a Lean Purse

### Cure #1: Start Thy Purse to Fattening
**Pay yourself first. Save at least 10% of everything you earn.**
- Before paying anyone else, set aside a portion for yourself
- "The coin I keep will grow into a golden stream"
- Live on 90% or less of your income
- The 10% is not missed because you adjust spending to what's left

### Cure #2: Control Thy Expenditures
**Budget your expenses so your necessities don't exceed 90% of income.**
- "What we call 'necessary expenses' will always grow to equal our incomes unless we protest to the contrary"
- Distinguish wants from needs
- Most spending is on desires, not necessities
- The person who spends less than they earn is already wealthy in discipline

### Cure #3: Make Thy Gold Multiply
**Invest your savings so they generate more wealth.**
- Savings sitting idle = losing value to inflation
- Put money to work earning interest, dividends, returns
- "Every piece of gold you save is a slave to work for you"
- Compound interest: "the eighth wonder of the world"

### Cure #4: Guard Thy Treasures from Loss
**Protect your capital. Seek advice only from those competent to give it.**
- "The first principle of investment is security for thy principal"
- Avoid get-rich-quick schemes
- Don't invest in things you don't understand
- Seek counsel from those who are already wealthy in that domain
- "Better a little caution than a great regret"

### Cure #5: Make of Thy Dwelling a Profitable Investment
**Own your home instead of renting.**
- A home is an asset that appreciates
- Rent = money spent with no return
- Homeownership = forced savings + appreciation

### Cure #6: Insure a Future Income
**Provide for your future and your family's future.**
- Build passive income streams
- Plan for retirement before you need to
- "Prepare for the days when you can no longer work"
- Insurance and long-term investments

### Cure #7: Increase Thy Ability to Earn
**Continuously improve your skills and value.**
- "The more wisdom you acquire, the more you can earn"
- Learn new skills, refine existing ones
- "Cultivate thy own powers, to study and become wiser, to be more skillful"
- Your earning ability is your greatest asset

## The Five Laws of Gold

### Law I: Gold cometh gladly and in increasing quantity to any man who will put not less than one-tenth of his earnings to create an estate for his future and that of his family.
**Save 10% consistently. Wealth begins with the habit of saving.**

### Law II: Gold laboreth diligently and contentedly for the wise owner who finds for it profitable employment, multiplying even as the flocks of the field.
**Invest wisely. Let compound growth work over time.**

### Law III: Gold clingeth to the protection of the cautious owner who invests it under the advice of men wise in its handling.
**Seek expert advice. Protect your principal.**

### Law IV: Gold slippeth away from the man who invests it in businesses or purposes with which he is not familiar or which are not approved by those skilled in keeping gold.
**Don't invest in what you don't understand.**

### Law V: Gold flees the man who would force it to impossible earnings or who followeth the alluring advice of tricksters and schemers or who trusts it to his own inexperience and romantic desires in investment.
**Avoid get-rich-quick schemes and unrealistic returns.**

## Additional Parable Wisdom

### The Man Who Desired Gold
Arkad, the richest man in Babylon, started as a poor scribe. He:
1. Noticed scribes who worked faster earned more
2. Saved 10% of everything he earned
3. Learned to live on less
4. Invested his savings
5. Learned from his mistakes (including a bad jewelry investment)
6. Kept learning and improving
7. Eventually managed the wealth of the money lender who taught him

### Meet the Goddess of Good Luck
Good luck comes to those who **accept opportunity**:
- "Good luck waits to come to that man who accepts opportunity"
- "Action is the key to any success"
- Procrastination = missed opportunity
- The man who acts decisively creates his own luck

### The Walls of Babylon
Babylon became wealthy not by luck, but by:
- Protecting what they built (walls)
- Working hard
- Trading skillfully
- Building systems that lasted
- "Babylon was built not on natural resources but on work and ingenuity"
