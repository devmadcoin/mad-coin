**The bot-generated knowledge dumps are your trivial many.** They feel productive. They're not.

## NEW CONTENT HIERARCHY

### Tier 1: ALWAYS DO (The 20%)
**Frequency: 60-70% of posts**

1. **Image + 1-2 sentence punch**
   - Every post that matters has a visual
   - Sentence count: 1-2 max. 3 = death.
   - The image IS the content. Text just labels it.

2. **Competitor / Rivalry Callouts**
   - Tag other projects
   - Create conversation (not just statements)
   - Let others defend themselves = reply velocity

3. **Proof-of-Action Posts**
   - Community wins
   - Burns
   - Locks
   - Game updates
   - Holder milestones
   - ANYTHING tangible

---

### Tier 2: SOMETIMES DO (The next 15%)
**Frequency: 20-30% of posts**

1. **Raw conviction one-liners**
   - "You are not your portfolio. You are your conviction." (131 imp, 10.7% — works because it's ONE line)
   - "The time is to be $MAD and bullish" + image (314 imp, 11.5%)

2. **Stage 2 Mirror Posts (the validated format)**
   - "Your 401k is BlackRock's asset..." (167 imp, 7.8% — the hook)
   - Keep under 280 chars for the hook tweet
   - Thread format: Hook → Bridge → Contract

---

### Tier 3: RARELY DO (The bottom 10%)
**Frequency: 0-10% of posts**

1. **Long philosophy threads**
   - Only when you have a SPECIFIC visual to pair
   - Never text-only
   - Never more than 3 sentences

2. **Framework explanations**
   - Pareto, Wyckoff, etc. — these are Telegram content, not X
   - X = visual punch. Telegram = depth.

3. **Generic hype**
   - "LETS GOOOO" — no visual, no proof = no engagement

## THE FORMAT RULES

### The $MAD X Post Formula

```
[IMAGE] — required, not optional
[1-2 sentences] — max 280 chars total
[Optional: single emoji or $MAD tag]
[NO hashtags] — deprecated, spam filter triggers
[NO external links in first tweet] — -50% reach
```

### What Makes an Image Work

1. **$MAD mascot / brand elements** — instant recognition
2. **Screenshots of proof** — burns, locks, wins
3. **Meme format** — recognizable template + $MAD twist
4. **Dark background + red/yellow** — brand colors
5. **Typography-only** — when the text IS the image

### What Kills an Image

1. Generic stock photos
2. Overcrowded text
3. No brand colors
4. Low contrast
5. Busy backgrounds

## POSTING FREQUENCY (Pareto-Applied)

**Old:** 8 posts/day (every 3 hours) = most get ignored
**New:** 4 posts/day (every 6 hours) = only Tier 1 content

**Math:**
- 4 Tier 1 posts × 12% avg engagement = 48% total engagement
- 8 mixed posts × 5% avg engagement = 40% total engagement
- **Less work. More results.**

**Peak hours (ET):**
- 8-10am — morning scroll
- 12-2pm — lunch break peak
- 5-7pm — evening commute
- 9-11pm — night scroll

**Post at these windows. Skip the rest.**

## TELEGRAM vs X SPLIT

**X = Visual Punch**
- 1-2 sentences
- Images required
- Proof > philosophy
- Conversation starters
- Peak hours only

**Telegram = Depth**
- Knowledge drops
- Framework explanations
- Community discussion
- Longer form allowed
- Bot philosophy welcome

**Don't cross the streams.** What works on Telegram tanks on X. What works on X feels shallow on Telegram. Design for the platform.

---

## THE MANDATE

**Every post must pass this test:**

> Would someone scroll past this and NOT stop?

If yes → don't post it.

> Is there proof, rivalry, or a visual punch?

If no → don't post it.

> Can I say this in 2 sentences or less?

If no → cut it in half.

---

## MEASUREMENT

**Track weekly:**
- Top 5 posts by engagement rate
- What do they have in common?
- Bottom 5 posts by engagement rate
- What do they have in common?

**Adjust monthly:**
- The 20% shifts. What worked in May might not work in July.
- Keep analyzing. Keep cutting the 80%.
