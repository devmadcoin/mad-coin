## Overview
Mario is the best-selling video game franchise of all time with 800M+ units sold across all titles. Created by Shigeru Miyamoto, Mario has appeared in over 200 games across every Nintendo platform. The franchise spans platformers, RPGs, racing, sports, party games, and spin-offs.

## Character Origins

### Mario
- **Original Name:** Jumpman (Donkey Kong, 1981)
- **Renamed:** Mario (Mario Bros., 1983) — after Mario Segale, Nintendo of America's landlord
- **Creator:** Shigeru Miyamoto
- **Design Origin (Critical):** Hardware limitations of 1980s directly shaped iconic look:
  - **Overalls:** Shirt + overalls = arms visible when moving (solid shirt would make arms "disappear")
  - **Mustache:** No pixels for a mouth — mustache implied mouth
  - **Big Nose:** Face readable in limited pixel space
  - **Cap:** No animation budget for hair — cap solved it instantly
  - **Gloves:** Added by artist Zavier Leslie Cabarga for North American Donkey Kong poster
  - **Stocky build:** Covered best in allocated sprite space
- **Occupation:** Plumber (established in Mario Bros., 1983)
- **Brother:** Luigi (younger but taller fraternal twin)
- **Love Interest:** Princess Peach
- **Arch-Nemesis:** Bowser

### Design Philosophy
Mario's blank-slate personality is intentional — Nintendo's philosophy is that a silent, straightforward character fits many genres and roles. This is why Mario works in platformers, racing, sports, RPGs, and party games without feeling out of character.

## Major Series

### Super Mario (Main Platformers)

#### 2D Era
- **Super Mario Bros.** (1985, NES) — saved the industry post-1983 crash
- **Super Mario Bros. 3** (1988, NES) — power-ups, world map, inventory
- **Super Mario World** (1990, SNES) — introduced Yoshi, cape feather
- **New Super Mario Bros.** (2006, DS) — 2.5D revival after 14-year gap
- **Super Mario Bros. Wonder** (2023, Switch) — latest 2D entry, Wonder Flower mechanic

#### 3D Era
- **Super Mario 64** (1996, N64) — invented 3D platforming; collectathon formula
- **Super Mario Sunshine** (2002, GameCube) — Isle Delfino, FLUDD water pack, Shadow Mario
- **Super Mario Galaxy** (2007, Wii) — gravity mechanics, spherical planets, orchestral score
- **Super Mario Galaxy 2** (2010, Wii) — refined Galaxy, Yoshi returns
- **Super Mario 3D Land** (2011, 3DS) — hybrid 2D/3D design
- **Super Mario 3D World** (2013, Wii U) — multiplayer 3D platformer
- **Super Mario Odyssey** (2017, Switch) — Cappy possession mechanic, open-world kingdoms

### Mario Kart
- **Debut:** Super Mario Kart (1992, SNES)
- **Style:** Kart racing with weapons/items
- **Key Innovation:** Blue Shell — comeback mechanic that punishes first place
- **Entries:** 8 main games + arcade versions + Tour (mobile)
- **Mario Kart 8 Deluxe** (Switch) — best-selling Switch game (~60M units)
- **Mario Kart World** (2025, Switch 2) — open-world racing

### Mario Party
- **Debut:** Mario Party (1998, N64)
- **Style:** Board game + mini-games
- **Formula:** Roll dice, move around board, compete in mini-games for coins/stars
- **Entries:** 15+ main games, multiple handheld versions
- **Controversy:** N64 era mini-games caused palm blisters (rotate stick rapidly)

### RPG Series

#### Paper Mario
- **Debut:** Paper Mario (2000, N64)
- **Style:** Paper-craft aesthetic, turn-based RPG
- **Evolution:** Original had traditional RPG mechanics; later entries shifted to action commands and sticker/card mechanics
- **Key Titles:** The Thousand-Year Door (GameCube, widely considered best), Color Splash, The Origami King

#### Mario & Luigi
- **Developer:** AlphaDream
- **Debut:** Superstar Saga (2003, GBA)
- **Style:** Action-command RPG with bro. attacks
- **Status:** AlphaDream went bankrupt in 2019; series on hold
- **Key Titles:** Bowser's Inside Story (2009, DS — widely considered best)

### Sports Games
- **Mario Golf** — arcade-style golf
- **Mario Tennis** — introduced Waluigi (N64)
- **Mario Strikers** — aggressive soccer/football
- **Mario Baseball** — Mario Superstar Baseball series

### Spin-Offs

#### Luigi's Mansion
- **Debut:** Luigi's Mansion (2001, GameCube — launch title)
- **Style:** Ghost-hunting with Poltergust vacuum
- **Story:** Luigi wins mansion in contest he never entered, Mario kidnapped
- **Key Titles:** Dark Moon (2013, 3DS), Luigi's Mansion 3 (2019, Switch)
- **Cultural Note:** "Year of Luigi" (2013) — Luigi's 30th anniversary celebration

#### Captain Toad
- **Debut:** Captain Toad: Treasure Tracker (2014, Wii U)
- **Spin-off from:** Super Mario 3D World bonus levels
- **Style:** Puzzle-platformer with diorama levels, Toad can't jump

#### Mario vs. Donkey Kong
- **Debut:** Mario vs. Donkey Kong (2004, GBA)
- **Style:** Puzzle-platformer, Lemmings-like — guide Mini Marios to exit

## Character Roster

### Core Heroes
- **Mario** — The hero, plumber, jumper
- **Luigi** — Taller, greener, more timid brother. "Year of Luigi" 2013
- **Princess Peach** — Ruler of Mushroom Kingdom, frequently kidnapped, occasionally playable protagonist (Super Princess Peach, 2005)
- **Princess Daisy** — Tomboyish ruler of Sarasaland, friend of Peach, first appeared in Super Mario Land (1989)
- **Rosalina** — Mysterious cosmic figure, watcher of cosmos, debuted in Super Mario Galaxy (2007)

### Sidekicks
- **Yoshi** — Dinosaur-like mount, long tongue for eating, spawned own franchise. Debut: Super Mario World (1990)
- **Toad** — Mushroom retainer, often serves as guide/shopkeeper
- **Toadette** — Female Toad, increasingly prominent (playable in New Super Mario Bros. U Deluxe, Captain Toad)
- **Cappy** — Sentient hat from Super Mario Odyssey (2017), possession mechanic

### Villains
- **Bowser (King Koopa)** — Main antagonist, King of Koopas, kidnaps Peach
- **Bowser Jr.** — Bowser's son, Shadow Mario in Sunshine, helps father
- **Kamek / Magikoopa** — Bowser's advisor/wizard
- **Wario** — Greedy anti-hero, Mario's doppelganger rival, starred in own franchise (Wario Land, WarioWare)
- **Waluigi** — Wario's accomplice, tennis/golf/party games staple, **not** in main platformers (internet meme about his exclusion)

### Other Recurring
- **Donkey Kong** — Originally Mario's rival (arcade), now hero of own franchise
- **Diddy Kong** — Donkey Kong's nephew
- **Birdo** — Pink dinosaur, originally antagonist, Yoshi's frequent ally
- **Professor E. Gadd** — Scientist inventor, created FLUDD, Poltergust, helps Luigi
- **Pauline** — Original damsel from Donkey Kong, revived as New Donk City mayor in Odyssey
- **Lumas** — Star creatures cared for by Rosalina

## The Mushroom Kingdom Universe
- **Central Location:** Mushroom Kingdom, ruled by Princess Peach
- **Landscape:** Diverse — forests, deserts, snowlands, beaches, mountains, plains, volcanos
- **Bowser's Territory:** Usually volcanic, his castle at the end of worlds
- **Other Locations:**
  - **Sarasaland** — Daisy's kingdom (Super Mario Land)
  - **Isle Delfino** — Tropical resort (Sunshine)
  - **New Donk City** — Realistic city (Odyssey)
  - **Comet Observatory** — Rosalina's base (Galaxy)

## Cultural Impact
- **Industry Savior:** Super Mario Bros. (1985) revived North American video game market after 1983 crash
- **Most Recognizable Character:** Mario is as globally recognizable as Mickey Mouse
- **Genre Innovation:** Created/influenced platformer genre, 3D collectathon, kart racing, party games
- **Mario Myths:**
  - "Luigi is dead" (Mansion theory)
  - "Waluigi will never be in Smash" (he's an Assist Trophy)
  - "Mario is evil" (fan theories about his treatment of enemies)
  - "The princess is in another castle" — Toad's iconic line from SMB
- **Crossover Dominance:** Super Smash Bros. roster anchor; Mario characters appear in more crossover games than any other franchise

## Sales Records
- **Super Mario Bros.:** 40.24 million units (NES) — one of best-selling games ever
- **Mario Kart 8 Deluxe:** ~60 million units (Switch) — best-selling Switch game
- **Total Mario Franchise:** 800+ million units across all games
- **Franchise Value:** Among the most valuable IP in entertainment

## Key Creators
- **Shigeru Miyamoto:** Creator of Mario, still involved in franchise direction
- **Koji Kondo:** Composer of iconic music (Super Mario Bros. theme, Zelda themes) — still active
- **Yoshiaki Koizumi:** Director of Super Mario Galaxy, Odyssey; current Senior Officer at Nintendo
- **Takashi Tezuka:** Co-creator of Mario, Zelda; producer on modern Mario games
