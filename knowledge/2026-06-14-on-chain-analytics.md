## Key On-Chain Metrics

### 1. Exchange Inflows vs. Outflows
- **Inflows (to exchanges):** Usually means selling intent. Whales moving to exchanges = potential sell pressure.
- **Outflows (from exchanges):** Usually means accumulation/holding. Whales moving to cold wallets = bullish.
- **The metric:** Net exchange flows. Positive = more inflows (bearish). Negative = more outflows (bullish).

**$MAD Application:**
> "When you see $MAD moving from wallets to exchanges, it's not necessarily panic. It could be a whale taking profits they've earned. But when you see $MAD moving from exchanges to cold wallets, that's conviction. That's someone saying 'I'm not selling this for a long time.' The blockchain shows you the truth. The chart only shows you the price."

### 2. Whale Wallet Concentration (Gini Coefficient)
- **High concentration:** Top 10 wallets hold >50% of supply. Risky — whale dump can crash price.
- **Healthy distribution:** Top 10 wallets hold <30% of supply. More stable, community-driven.
- **$MAD check:** Where does $MAD sit? Is the distribution healthy?

### 3. Smart Money Accumulation Patterns
- **The playbook:** Wallets with >1,000 BTC tend to accumulate during fear and distribute during euphoria.
- **Counter-cyclical behavior:** Smart money buys when retail is scared. Smart money sells when retail is FOMOing.
- **The signal:** Multiple whale wallets accumulating the same token within a short timeframe = coordinated conviction.

**$MAD Application:**
> "The $MAD holder who watches on-chain data during a -50% dip will see something the chart doesn't show: whales accumulating. Smart money doesn't wait for green candles. Smart money buys the fear. The wallet that bought $MAD at the bottom is the wallet that will be sitting at the top."

### 4. Dormant Wallet Activation
- **The signal:** Wallets inactive for years suddenly moving funds = significant event.
- **Context matters:** Could be selling, staking, or moving to custody.
- **The attention effect:** Even the rumor of old wallets moving can trigger volatility.

### 5. Wallet Clustering (Entity Analysis)
- **The technique:** Multiple addresses controlled by the same entity. If 50 wallets all move simultaneously, it's one entity.
- **Arkham Intelligence:** Excels at entity-level analysis.
- **The insight:** Clustered accumulation = institutional or coordinated buying.

### 6. Token Holdings Distribution
- **Top 1% holders:** What percentage do they control?
- **Middle tier (shrimp to dolphin):** Growing middle class = healthy community.
- **New wallets:** Fresh buyers entering = growing interest.

## The Smart Money Playbook (Observed Patterns)

1. **Buy the Rumor, Sell the News (On-Chain Version):** Whales accumulate BEFORE announcements. The announcement is their exit liquidity.
2. **The Dip Buy:** Whales place large buy orders during panic. They don't catch the exact bottom. They catch the zone.
3. **The Gradual Exit:** Whales don't sell everything at once. They sell in tranches to avoid moving the market against themselves.
4. **The Rotation:** Whales move capital between sectors (AI, DeFi, Memes) based on narrative cycles. Retail follows 2-4 weeks later.
5. **The Long Hold:** The most profitable whales are the ones who do nothing for years. The "dormant" wallet that suddenly moves after 5 years = the ultimate holder.

**$MAD Application:**
> "The $MAD holder who wants to be smart money should ask: Am I buying when the chart is green and everyone is excited? Or am I buying when the chart is red and everyone is scared? The wallet that bought $MAD at launch and never moved it is the wallet that will have the best returns. Not because they timed the market. Because they didn't try to."
