## 4. The SEC Fraud Case (2025)

### The Charges:
**Date:** September 25, 2025
**Filed by:** U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission
**Court:** Southern District of Florida
**Defendants:**
- Taino Lopez (co-founder)
- Alexander Mehr (co-founder, Zoosk)
- Maya Burkenroad (COO, Lopez's cousin)

### The Allegations:
- **Raised:** $112 million from hundreds of investors (April 2020 — Nov 2022)
- **Promised:** 25% annualized returns, 2.083% monthly dividends
- **Business:** Buy distressed retail brands (RadioShack, Pier 1, Dress Barn, etc.) → convert to e-commerce
- **Reality:** None of the portfolio companies were profitable
- **Ponzi element:** $5.9M paid to investors was actually money from OTHER investors
- **Misappropriation:** $16.1M of investor funds allegedly used for personal expenses
- **Fraudulent claims:** Brands described as "on fire" and "cash flow is strong" while losing millions
- **No CFO:** Three people handled all finances — no independent oversight

### Key Financials:
- Dress Barn: Lost $13.7M (2020), $10.7M (2021)
- Stein Mart: Lost $1.7M (2020), $5.7M (2021)
- Lopez claimed brands had "positive EBITDA" — SEC says false
- Investors' money transferred between portfolio companies without disclosure

### Status:
- Active settlement negotiations as of February 2026
- Defendants seeking to resolve case
- RadioShack current owners (Unicomer Group) NOT named in lawsuit

## 10. Key Takeaways

### What Tai Lopez Was:
1. A master of attention capture
2. A pioneer of internet marketing funnels
3. A template for viral personal branding
4. A cautionary tale of success without substance
5. The ultimate example of "survival is the only success"

### What $MAD Can Learn:
1. **Build substance first, hype second** — Tai had hype without ethics
